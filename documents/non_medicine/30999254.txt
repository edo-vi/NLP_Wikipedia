The '''''Cinebox''''' was a coin-operated Italian 16mm [[Movie projector|film projector]] [[jukebox]] type machine invented in 1959 that appeared in Europe to rival the French made [[Scopitone]]<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.scopitonearchive.com/cinebox/index.html |title=Cinebox/Colorama/Cinejukebox |publisher=Scopitone Archive |date= |accessdate=2014-02-24}}</ref>  that appeared in 1960.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.michelebovi.it/?p=1965&lang=en |title=Michele BoviThe Spy-Story of Music Videos * Michele Bovi |publisher=Michelebovi.it |accessdate=2014-02-24 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140219173415/http://www.michelebovi.it/?p=1965&lang=en |archive-date=2014-02-19 |url-status=dead }}</ref> The Cinebox was manufactured in Rome by [[Ottico Meccanica Italiana]].

In 1963 it appeared in the USA<ref>{{cite book|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=lPYGAQAAIAAJ&q=cinebox |title=Newsweek - Google Books |year=1963 |accessdate=2014-02-24}}</ref> and was retitled '''''Colorama''''' in 1965.  In 1961 Cinebox machines were placed on [[ocean liner]]s of the [[American Export Lines]] with Cineboxes showing cartoons and [[short subject]] comedies in on board nurseries whilst on board lounges showed musical and [[Travel documentary|travelogues]].  Each of the Cineboxes held five 40 minute films.<ref>{{cite book|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=dCAEAAAAMBAJ&q=%22the+three+stooges+go+around+the+world+on+eighty+cents%22&pg=PA53 |title=Billboard - Google Books |date=1961-09-25 |accessdate=2014-02-24}}</ref>

In the United States the Cinebox musical juke box provided three minutes of sound film for 25 cents with 40 titles available at a time on the machine.<ref>{{cite book|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=niADAAAAMBAJ&q=cinebox&pg=PA125 |title=Popular Science - Google Books |date= October 1963|accessdate=2014-02-24}}</ref>
