'''Peter Foley''' (born {{birth based on age as of date | 56 |2022| 3 | 18|noage=y}}) is an American  former [[U.S. Ski & Snowboard]] (USSS) [[snowboarding]] coach.<ref name="auto3">{{Cite web|url=https://www.espn.com/olympics/story/_/id/33539749/us-snowboard-head-coach-peter-foley-suspended-safesport-sexual-misconduct-allegations|title=U.S. Snowboard head coach Peter Foley suspended by SafeSport after sexual misconduct allegations|date=March 18, 2022|website=ESPN|author=Alyssa Roenigk and Tisha Thompson}}</ref><ref name="auto2">{{Cite web|url=https://www.espn.com/olympics/story/_/id/35582364/snowboarders-sue-ex-coach-peter-foley-federation-usopc-sex-trafficking-coverup|title=Olympians sue USOPC, others for sex trafficking|author=Alyssa Roenigk and Tisha Thompson|date=February 3, 2023|website=ESPN}}</ref> Foley served as the head coach of the U.S. Snowboard team starting with when it was founded in 1994, including at three Olympics. In 2001 he was named USSS  Coach of the Year, and in 2021 he was selected by USSS as Snowboard Coach of the Year. He was fired by USSS in March 2022, after [[sexual misconduct]] allegations were made. He had been coach of the U.S. snowboarding team for 28 years. On August 8, 2023, [[SafeSport]] suspended him for ten years for sexual misconduct.

==Biography==
===Early years===
Foley is the son of Terry Foley, a [[Bend, Oregon]] native, former [[alpine ski]] racer, and [[ski]] coach.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.bendsource.com/outside/a-serious-snow-jones-predicting-about-and-riding-into-winter-2134571|title=A Serious Snow Jones: Predicting about and riding into winter|first=Bob|last=Woodward|website=The Source Weekly – Bend|date=November 4, 2009}}</ref>  He grew up as a ski racer in [[Colorado]].<ref name="auto6"/> He initially attended the [[University of Colorado Boulder]], and then transferred to the [[University of Oregon]], where he studied journalism.<ref name="auto6">{{Cite web|url=https://www.mountainzone.com/snowboarding/olympics/teamusa/staff.html|title=US Snowboard Team: Coaches; Peter Foley; Head Coach|website=Mountain Zone}}</ref> He began [[snowboarding]] in the early 1990s, and raced as a professional for two seasons.<ref name="auto6"/> As of 2010 he lived in [[Mt. Hood, Oregon]], and in 2014 with his wife and two children in [[Hood River, Oregon]].<ref name="auto6"/><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.oregonlive.com/olympics/2010/01/olympic_snowboarders_love_to_t.html|title=Olympic snowboarders love to train at Mt. Hood, so why doesn't Oregon produce more Olympians of its own?|author=Rachel Bachman|date=January 30, 2010|website=Oregonlive}}</ref> He is also director of Product Development at Insight Replay, which he co-founded in 2013.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://insightreplay.com/about/|title=About Insight Replay; Peter Foley|website=Insight Replay}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.tampabay.com/sports/footballpreps/lennard-breaks-down-the-game-8230-during-the-game/2246849/|title=Lennard breaks down the game … during the game|website=Tampa Bay Times|author=Brandon Wright|date= September 23, 2015}}</ref>

=== Team USA snowboard team head coach===
Foley served as the head coach of the U.S. Snowboard team starting in 1994, when it was founded, including at three Olympics, through 2022.<ref>Eddie Pells (August 8, 2023). [https://www.seattletimes.com/sports/former-us-snowboard-coach-peter-foley-suspended-for-sexual-misconduct/ "Former US snowboard coach Peter Foley suspended for sexual misconduct,"] ''Seattle Times''.</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.espn.com/olympics/story/_/id/33267925/us-ski-snowboard-investigating-sexual-misconduct-racism-allegations|title=U.S. Ski & Snowboard investigating allegations of sexual misconduct and racial slurs|author=Alyssa Roenigk|date=February 11, 2022|website=ESPN}}</ref> By 2022, he had led it to seven [[Olympic Games]], at which team members won a combined 35 [[Olympic medal]]s.<ref name="auto3"/> Foley was in charge of seeding and deciding which athletes competed in [[World Cup]] competitions, in addition to the Olympic team.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://townlift.com/2022/03/multiple-women-accuse-ex-u-s-snowboard-coach-peter-foley-of-sexual-misconduct/|title=Multiple women accuse ex-U.S. Snowboard coach Peter Foley of sexual misconduct|first=Parker|last=Malatesta|date=March 28, 2022|website=TownLift, Park City News}}</ref> In 2001 he was named [[U.S. Ski & Snowboard]] (USSS) Coach of the Year, and in 2021 he was selected by USSS as Snowboard Coach of the Year.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://usskiandsnowboard.org/sport-development/coach-development/coach-year-awards|title=Coach of the Year Awards|website=U.S. Ski & Snowboard}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://usskiandsnowboard.org/news/sprang-norge-ski-club-honored-us-ski-snowboard-coach-club-year|title=Sprang, Norge Ski Club Honored As U.S. Ski & Snowboard Coach, Club Of The Year|website=U.S. Ski & Snowboard|date=May 18, 2021}}</ref>

===2022; Safety plan, dismissal as coach, temporary suspension===
In February 2022 former USSS team member and 2010 Olympian [[Callan Chythlook-Sifsof]] made social posts on Instagram during the [[Beijing Olympics 2022|2022 Beijing Winter Olympics]] accusing Foley of [[sexual misconduct]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2022/02/11/sports/olympics/peter-foley-snowboarding-coach-accusations.html|title=A U.S. Olympic snowboarding coach is under investigation for sexual misconduct.|author=John Branch|work=The New York Times|date=February 12, 2022}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.usatoday.com/story/sports/2022/03/22/women-allege-sexual-misconduct-snowboarding-coach-peter-foley-report/7126997001/|title=Report: Four women allege sexual misconduct by snowboarding coach Peter Foley|website=USA TODAY|date=March 22, 2022|author=Rachel Axon}}</ref><ref name="auto4">{{Cite web|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2022/03/22/sports/olympics/peter-foley-sexual-misconduct.html|title=Snowboarding Coach Faces New Accusations of Sexual Misconduct; Peter Foley, already accused of coercing athletes into posing for nude photos, now faces scrutiny from more women. He has been relieved of his duties as a U.S. Olympic coach.|author=John Branch |work=The New York Times|date=March 22, 2022}}</ref><ref name="auto3"/><ref name="auto2"/> She wrote: "I cannot watch another Olympic Games without saying this publicly."<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.sltrib.com/sports/2022/02/12/utah-snowboarder-faye/|title=Utah snowboarder Faye Gulini announces retirement, expresses support for embattled coach; On a day U.S. snowboarding wins gold, a former athlete accuses coach Peter Foley of inappropriate conduct toward women and building a toxic culture|website=The Salt Lake Tribune|author=Julie Jag|date=February 12, 2022}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.leparisien.fr/sports/JO/jo-dhiver-2022-lentraineur-de-snowboard-americain-peter-foley-accuse-de-harcelement-sexuel-12-02-2022-ZZURVYKXHNA7RJ4HLCWDSGUOUY.php|title=l’entraîneur de snowboard américain Peter Foley accusé de harcèlement sexuel|author=Maxime Ducher |date=February 12, 2022|website=Le Parisien}}</ref>  Foley denied the allegations vehemently.<ref name="auto4"/><ref name="auto3"/><ref name="auto5"/>  USSS immediately implemented an "athlete safety plan" that prohibited Foley from having "one-on-one interaction with female athletes," and from going into an athlete village.<ref name="auto3"/><ref name="auto2"/> 

USSS also immediately notified the [[U.S. Center for SafeSport]], an organization authorized by the [[U.S. Congress]] to police reports related to sexual abuse in U.S. Olympic sports, upon learning of Chythlook-Sifsof’s Instagram post.<ref name="auto7">{{Cite web|url=https://www.adn.com/sports/national-sports/2022/04/14/sex-abuse-case-involving-us-snowboard-coach-exposes-flaws-in-new-reporting-system/|title=Sex-abuse case involving U.S. snowboard coach exposes flaws in new reporting system|website=Anchorage Daily News|date=April 14, 2022|author=Eddie Pells}}</ref> But on February 16, SafeSport Center vice president Bobby Click emailed USSS that: "based on the information the center has, we have chosen not to implement any types of measure."<ref name="auto7"/> 

On February 21, 2022, USSS placed Foley on a leave of absence.<ref name="auto2"/><ref name="auto">{{Cite web|url=https://www.espn.com/olympics/snowboarding/story/_/id/33558337/peter-foley-us-snowboard-coach-sexual-misconduct-allegations|title=Foley out at U.S. Snowboard after sexual misconduct allegations|date=March 20, 2022|author=Alyssa Roenigk and Tisha Thompson|website=ESPN}}</ref> It fired him on March 20, 2022, days after the Beijing Olympics concluded.<ref name="auto5">{{Cite web|url=https://people.com/longtime-us-snowboard-coach-peter-foley-suspended-10-years-sexual-misconduct-7574640|title=Longtime U.S. Snowboard Coach Peter Foley Suspended 10 Years for Sexual Misconduct; The decision concludes an 18-month investigation stemming from accusations made by a former U.S. Olympic team member|website=People|author= Sean Neumann|date=August 9, 2023}}</ref><ref name="auto2"/><ref name="auto"/><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://usskiandsnowboard.org/news/statement-regarding-termination-us-snowboard-coach-peter-foley|title=Statement Regarding Termination Of U.S. Snowboard Coach Peter Foley|website=U.S. Ski & Snowboard|date=March 25, 2022}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.foxnews.com/sports/peter-foley-no-longer-employed-u-s-ski-snowboard-sexual-misconduct-allegations|title=Peter Foley no longer employed by US Ski and Snowboard following sexual misconduct allegations; Foley served as the coach of the US snowboard team since its inception in 1994|first=David|last=Aaro|date=March 20, 2022|website=Fox News}}</ref> He had been coach of the U.S. snowboarding team for 28 years.<ref name="auto5"/>

USSS said that it had reported the allegations to the [[United States Olympic & Paralympic Committee]] (USOPC), as well as to SafeSport.<ref name="auto2"/>

===2023; lawsuits, and 10-year suspension===
In February 2023, Olympic [[bronze medal]]ist [[Rosey Fletcher]] ([[parallel giant slalom]]) and other former USSS team members sued Foley, along with the national federation, its former CEO, and the USOPC, in [[U.S. District Court]] in Los Angeles for [[sex trafficking]], harassment, and enabling and covering up repeated acts of [[sexual assault]] and misconduct, alleging that the defendants "conspired and acted in concert with one another to commit unlawful acts."<ref name="auto2"/> They alleged that Foley exploited his position of trust to "coerce [[sexual act]]s through force, manipulation, emotional abuse, intimidation, and retaliation."<ref name="auto2"/> Fletcher said that Foley sexually assaulted her at a U.S. team camp when she was 19, and again at a post-race event at the Olympics.<ref name="auto2"/> Former national team member Erin O'Malley alleged that she was "sexually assaulted and harassed ... by Foley, who exploited their unequal power dynamic."<ref name="auto2"/> Foley and the other defendants asked the court to throw out the lawsuit; a hearing is set for October 2023.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://thebrunswicknews.com/sports/national_sports/former-team-usa-snowboard-coach-peter-foley-suspended-10-years-for-sexual-misconduct/article_2334f55d-29b1-5587-9315-bf29df8cdcef.html|title=Former Team USA snowboard coach Peter Foley suspended 10 years for sexual misconduct|author=Bill Shaikin|date=August 8, 2023|website=The Brunswick News}}</ref>

Separately in February 2023, the former USSS employee who had previously filed a report with Safesport filed a lawsuit against Foley and others for the alleged sexual assaults.<ref name="auto2"/><ref name="auto4"/><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.si.com/olympics/2022/03/22/peter-foley-accused-sexual-assault-us-snowboard-coach|title=Former U.S. snowboard coach Peter Foley accused of sexual assault |work= Sports Illustrated|author=Daniela Perez|date=March 22, 2022}}</ref>

On August 8, 2023, after an 18-month investigation, SafeSport suspended Foley for ten years for sexual misconduct.<ref name="auto5"/><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://uscenterforsafesport.org/response-and-resolution/centralized-disciplinary-database/|title=Centralized Disciplinary Database |publisher= U.S. Center for SafeSport}}</ref><ref>Les Carpenter (August 8, 2023). [https://www.washingtonpost.com/sports/olympics/2023/08/08/peter-foley-suspended-us-snowboard-coach/ "Olympics; Former U.S. snowboard coach Peter Foley suspended after sexual misconduct probe,"] ''The Washington Post''.</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.usatoday.com/story/sports/olympics/2023/08/08/safesport-suspends-former-usa-snowboarding-coach-peter-foley/70555789007/|title=SafeSport suspends ex-US Olympic snowboarding coach Peter Foley after sexual misconduct probe|website=USA TODAY|author=Tom Schad|date=August 8, 2023}}</ref>
