{{short description|Inactivated COVID-19 vaccine for carnivores}}
'''Carnivac-Cov'''<ref name="reuters-registered" /><ref name="wsj-start" /><ref name="vaccines-editorial" /> or '''Karnivak-Kov'''<ref name="guardian-covid-animals" /><ref name="vaccines-editorial" /> is the first [[Animal vaccination|veterinary vaccine]] against [[COVID-19]].<ref name="vaccines-editorial">{{Cite journal |vauthors=Chavda VP, Feehan J, Apostolopoulos V |date=10 June 2021 |title=A Veterinary Vaccine for SARS-CoV-2: The First COVID-19 Vaccine for Animals |journal=Vaccines |volume=9 |issue=6 |pages=631 |doi=10.3390/vaccines9060631 |pmc=8228738 |pmid=34200587|hdl=11343/287299 |doi-access=free }}</ref> It is an [[inactivated vaccine]] for [[carnivore]]s such as [[dog]]s, [[cat]]s, [[fox]]es, [[arctic fox]]es and [[mink]].<ref name="reuters-registered" /> It was developed by the [[Federal Service for Veterinary and Phytosanitary Supervision (Russia)|Federal Service for Veterinary and Phytosanitary Supervision of Russia]]<ref>{{cite news |title=Russia launches vaccination of animals against COVID-19 |url=https://tass.com/society/1294507 |access-date=21 January 2022 |work=TASS |date=27 May 2021}}</ref> to help prevent mutations in animals that can occur in [[cross-species transmission]],<ref name="reuters-registered" /><ref name="vaccines-editorial" /> as is suspected to have occurred with the [[Cluster 5]] variant in Denmark in November 2020.<ref name="guardian-covid-animals">{{cite news |title=From hippos to hamsters: how Covid is affecting creatures great and small |url=https://www.theguardian.com/world/2021/dec/11/from-hippos-to-hamsters-how-covid-is-affecting-creatures-great-and-small |access-date=21 January 2022 |work=The Guardian |date=11 December 2021}}</ref>

The vaccine is given in 2 doses 21 days apart.<ref name="wsj-start">{{cite news |title=Russia Rolls Out Covid-19 Vaccine for Animals |url=https://www.wsj.com/articles/russia-rolls-out-covid-19-vaccine-for-animals-11622128581 |access-date=21 January 2022 |work=The Wall Street Journal |date=27 May 2021}}</ref> In trials, the tested animals sustained an immune response for at least six months.<ref name="reuters-registered" /> The full course costs 500&nbsp;[[Russian ruble|â‚½]] ([[United States dollar|US$]]7).<ref name="wsj-start" />

Clinical trials on animals began in October 2020.<ref>{{cite news |title=Russia registers 'world's first' COVID-19 vaccine for animals |url=https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2021/3/31/russia-registers-worlds-first-covid-19-vaccine-for-animals |access-date=21 January 2022 |work=Al Jazeera |date=31 March 2021}}</ref> The vaccine was registered in Russia on 31 March 2021,<ref name="reuters-registered">{{cite news |title=Russia registers world's first COVID-19 vaccine for animals |url=https://www.reuters.com/business/healthcare-pharmaceuticals/russia-registers-worlds-first-covid-19-vaccine-animals-watchdog-2021-03-31/ |access-date=21 January 2022 |work=Reuters |date=31 March 2021 |location=Moscow}}</ref> where production began on 30 April<ref name="tass-production">{{cite news |title=Russia launches production of coronavirus vaccine for animals |url=https://tass.com/society/1285195 |access-date=21 January 2022 |work=TASS |date=30 April 2021}}</ref> and administration began on 26 May.<ref>{{cite news |title=Covid: Russia starts vaccinating animals |url=https://www.bbc.com/news/world-europe-57259961 |access-date=21 January 2022 |work=BBC News |date=26 May 2021}}</ref>
