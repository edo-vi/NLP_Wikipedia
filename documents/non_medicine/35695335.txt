'''Carmelite water''' is an [[ethanol|alcoholic]] extract of [[lemon balm]] and other herbs. It was initially crafted in the 14th century by [[Carmelites|Carmelite]] nuns from the Abbey of St Just,<ref>{{cite book | title=The new perfume handbook | publisher=Springer | author=Groom, Nigel | year=1997 | pages=448 | isbn=0751404039}}</ref> and was commercialized under the name ''Eau de Carmes''.<ref>{{cite web | url=http://www.mayonews.ie/index.php?option=com_content&view=article&id=10742:food-using-lemon-balm-in-the-kitchen&catid=74:tasting&Itemid=100028 | title=FOOD Using lemon balm in the kitchen | newspaper=The Mayo News | date=September 6, 2010 | accessdate=May 2, 2012 | author=Hiller, Sabine}}</ref> It is used as an [[herbal tonic]] and [[toilet water]].<ref>{{cite book | title=Encyclopædia Britannica: Or, A Dictionary of Arts..., Volume 14, Part 1 | year=1797}}</ref><ref>{{cite web | url=https://www.theguardian.com/society/2003/apr/26/features.health17 | title=Cream of the crop | newspaper=The Guardian | date=April 26, 2003 | accessdate=May 2, 2012}}</ref>
