'''Stefan Falimierz''', or '''Stefan Falimirz''', was a [[Polish people|Polish]] [[physician]] and [[botanist]]. He served at the court of the [[voivode]] Jan Tęczyński in [[Kraśnik]]. Falimierz is known for his 1534 work ''On Herbs and Their Potency'' (''O ziolach y o moczy gich'').<ref>{{cite book
|title=Studies in Polish Civilization
|year=1971
|publisher=Institute on East Central Europe, Columbia University
|pages=347}}</ref>  The book has been described as "one of the most lavishly illustrated volumes published in Poland in the early sixteenth century" and "also the first real encyclopedia of natural science in Polish".<ref>{{cite web | url = https://folgerpedia.folger.edu/The_Werner_Gundersheimer_Conservation_Laboratory#Saving_Old_Notes_in_a_Polish_Herbal | title = Saving Old Notes in a Polish Herbal | accessdate = 2023-03-05 | publisher = [[Folger Shakespeare Library]]}}</ref> Among other topics, it includes recipes for preparing various types of [[nalewka]].
