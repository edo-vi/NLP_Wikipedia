'''Amy Foxx-Orenstein, [[Doctor of Osteopathic Medicine|D.O.]], [[American College of Gastroenterology|FACG]]''' is an American [[Gastroenterology|gastroenterologist]]. She is an Associate Professor of Medicine at the [[Mayo Clinic College of Medicine]] a former president of the [[American College of Gastroenterology]] (2007â€“2008). Dr. Foxx-Orenstein is a fellow of the [[American College of Physicians]] as well as a fellow of the American College of Gastroenterology.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://gi.org/media/about-acg/ |title=About ACG &#124; American College of Gastroenterology |publisher=Gi.org |date= |accessdate=2012-05-26 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190203085053/http://gi.org/media/about-acg/ |archive-date=2019-02-03 |url-status=dead }}</ref> She attended medical school at [[Des Moines University]] in [[Des Moines, Iowa]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.mayoclinic.org/biographies/foxx-orenstein-amy-e-d-o/bio-20053433 |title=Researcher Profile: Foxx-Orenstein, Amy E, D.O |publisher=Mayo Clinic |accessdate=5 Nov 2015}}</ref> She completed [[internal medicine]] residency training at [[Geisinger Health System]] in Pennsylvania and gastroenterology fellowship at [[Virginia Commonwealth University]] Health System.<ref>{{cite web |title=Dr. Amy E. Foxx Orenstein, DO |url=https://health.usnews.com/doctors/amy-foxx-orenstein-256144#experience |website=U.S. News & World Report}}</ref>

Dr. Foxx-Orenstein is the author of numerous scholarly articles on [[gastroenterology]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/sites/entrez?cmd=PureSearch&db=pubmed&term=Foxx-Orenstein%20AE%5BAuthor%5D |title=Foxx-Orenstein AE[Author&#93; - PubMed - NCBI |publisher=Ncbi.nlm.nih.gov |date=2012-04-04 |accessdate=2012-05-26}}</ref>
