'''John Luther Zimmerman IV''' (born November 26, 1973) is an American professional [[pair skater]] and coach. With skating partner [[Kyoko Ina]], he is the [[2002 World Figure Skating Championships|2002 World]] bronze medalist and a three-time [[U.S. Figure Skating Championships|U.S. national]] champion. They also competed at the 2002 Olympics. Zimmerman was suspended by the [[United States Center for SafeSport]] in March 2021 for a two-year period.

==Personal life==
Zimmerman was born in [[Birmingham, Alabama]].<ref name=ISU-0102/> He has two older sisters. He married Italian-American skater [[Silvia Fontana]] on August 28, 2003.<ref name=5FT/> They have two daughters, one born on April 2, 2012, at Northwest Medical Center in [[Coconut Creek, Florida]],<ref name=IN120411/> and another born on June 2, 2013.<ref>{{cite tweet|user=DavidBaden|author=David Baden|number=341661835767975936|date=3 June 2013|title=Congrats #SilviaFontana &amp; @skaterzimmerman on birth of Eva Zimmerman Born 6/2/13 7p 6oz All healthy/happy! @icenetwork @usfigureskating}}</ref>

==Career==
Zimmerman started skating at age 3 at a mall. He briefly partnered with Brie Teaboldt for the 1994-95 season. Then he paired with [[Stephanie Stiegler]] from 1995 through 1998, and won the bronze medal at the [[1997 U.S. Figure Skating Championships]] while being coached by [[Peter Oppegard]]. Their partnership ended in 1998 due to injuries.<ref name=JBM1998/>

Zimmerman teamed up with [[Kyoko Ina]] in 1998.<ref name=NYT020107/> Initially, they were coached by Peter Burrows and Mary Lynn Gelderman in [[Monsey, New York]], and they also commuted to [[Stamford, Connecticut]], to work with [[Tamara Moskvina]].<ref name=JBM1998/> They later trained under Moskvina and [[Igor Moskvin]] in [[Hackensack, New Jersey|Hackensack]], [[New Jersey]].<ref name=ISU-0102/>

Ina and Zimmerman won the bronze medal at the [[2002 World Figure Skating Championships|2002 World Championships]]. In 2003, they turned professional and began skating on [[Stars on Ice]].

Zimmerman competed in the January 2006 [[Fox Broadcasting Company|FOX]] television program "[[Skating with Celebrities]]", where he partnered with FOX broadcaster [[Jillian Barberie]]. They finished in second place.

Zimmerman was featured as Yahoo's special guest expert correspondent for figure skating at the [[Figure skating at the 2006 Winter Olympics|2006 Winter Olympics]] in [[Turin]].<ref>{{cite web |url= https://sports.yahoo.com/olympics/torino2006/expertsarchive?author=John+Zimmerman |title= Turin 2006 Winter Olympics - Expert Archive |publisher= Yahoo! Sports |archiveurl= https://web.archive.org/web/20060429230023/http://sports.yahoo.com/olympics/torino2006/expertsarchive?author=John+Zimmerman |archivedate= 2006-04-29}}</ref> He later competed in an [[American Broadcasting Company|ABC]] skating series "Thin Ice" (aired on March 19, 2010), paired with world champion Canadian ice dancer [[Shae-Lynn Bourne]]. They finished in second place, winning a total of $50,000. They skated to "Closer" by [[Ne-Yo]] and "Poker Face" by [[Lady Gaga]].

Zimmerman worked as a coach at Panthers Ice Den in [[Coral Springs, Florida]], with Silvia Fontana.<ref name=IN130517/> They now coach at AdventHealth Center Ice in [[Wesley Chapel, Florida]].<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.floridahospitalcenterice.com/sports/coach-bios/ |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170826142913/http://www.floridahospitalcenterice.com/sports/coach-bios |archive-date=2017-08-26 |title=Coaches' Bios {{!}} Florida Hospital Center Ice}}</ref> He has coached [[Haven Denney]] / [[Brandon Frazier]] (from Autumn 2012 to February 2015 and since 2018 again)<ref name=IN130517/><ref name=ISU-HDBF/> and [[Vanessa James]] / [[Morgan Cipres]] (from June 2016).<ref name=IN170125/>

==Suspension==
In December 2019, Zimmerman, Fontana, and fellow coach Vinny Dispenza, were accused in a [[United States Center for SafeSport]] investigation of covering up alleged [[sexual abuse]] committed by Ciprès via intimidation tactics.<ref name=USAT-121019>{{cite web|url=https://www.usatoday.com/story/sports/olympics/2019/12/10/sexual-abuse-olympic-figure-skater-coaches-being-investigated/2629777001/|title=Olympic figure skater, coaches being investigated for sexual abuse allegations|first=Christine|last=Brennan|author-link=Christine Brennan|publisher=[[USA Today]]|date=December 10, 2019}}</ref> Zimmerman was suspended for two years and received one additional year of probation by SafeSport in March 2021 for abuse of process, emotional misconduct, and failure to report sexual abuse of a 13 year old he was coaching.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://olympics.nbcsports.com/2021/03/10/john-zimmerman-figure-skating-ban-safe-sport/|title = John Zimmerman, figure skating coach, Olympian, banned by SafeSport|date = 10 March 2021}}</ref> He was barred from any skating events run by U.S. Figure Skating or the Olympic committee, and prohibited from attending any facility run by those groups.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://olympics.nbcsports.com/2021/03/10/john-zimmerman-figure-skating-ban-safe-sport/|title=John Zimmerman, figure skating coach, Olympian, banned by SafeSport|agency=Associated Press|date=March 10, 2021}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.usatoday.com/story/sports/olympics/2021/03/09/former-olympic-skater-suspended-after-accusation-sex-abuse-cover-up/4635608001/|title=Former Olympic skater John Zimmerman suspended following accusation of sex abuse cover-up|first=Christine|last=Brennan|website=USA TODAY}}</ref>
