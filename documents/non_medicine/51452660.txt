'''Doktor Koster's Antigaspills''' were an early 20th century [[alternative medicine|alternative medication]] intended to treat stomach upset and excessive [[flatulence]]. They are best known for being administered to [[Adolf Hitler]] by his physician, [[Theodor Morell]], to treat Hitler's stomach ailments.<ref name="eb1">{{cite book|last=Lambert|first=Angela|title=The Lost Life of Eva Braun|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=XgQDBAAAQBAJ&q=Koster%27s+Antigaspills&pg=PA262|date=September 3, 2014|publisher=St. Martin's Press|isbn=978-1466879966|page=262}}</ref> Morrell, regarded as a [[quack medicine|quack]] by Hitler's associates, administered a wide variety of unorthodox concoctions and medications to Hitler beginning in 1936.<ref name="Edinburgh">{{cite journal|last=Doyle|first=D|title=Adolf Hitler's Medical Care|year=2005|journal=JR Coll Edinb|volume=35|issue=35|pages=75â€“82|pmid=15825245|url=https://infam.antville.org/static/sites/infam/files/hitlers_medical_care.pdf}}</ref>

The pill's active ingredients consisted primarily of [[atropine]] (an extract of ''[[Atropa belladonna]]'') and [[strychnine]].<ref name="Edinburgh"/>
