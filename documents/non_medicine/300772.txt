'''Madhava''' (or '''Madhava-kara''') was a 7th-century or early 8th-century [[India]]n [[Ayurveda]] practitioner who wrote the ''Rug-vinischaya'', also known as the ''Madhava Nidana'', which soon assumed a position of authority. In the 79 chapters of this book, he lists diseases along with their causes, symptoms, and complications. He also included a chapter on [[smallpox]] (masūrikā).<ref name=Hopkins/><ref name=Nicholas/><ref name=Rao/>
