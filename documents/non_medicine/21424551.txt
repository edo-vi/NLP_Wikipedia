The National Literary Awards defines '''independent''' or '''"indie" literature''' as "books published outside mainstream publishing."[http://nationalliteraryawards.com/americanbookchoiceaward/index.htm]{{Dead link|date=January 2020 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=yes }} Such books are rarely recognized and hard to pin down, but some examples include "Damastor" by [[Dimitri Iatrou]],  "Returning Home" by Marcus Blake and "Hope...Joy (and a Few Little Thoughts) for Pregnant Teens" by [[Rachel Brigoni]].
