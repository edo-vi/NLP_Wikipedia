'''''Head Games: The Global Concussion Crisis''''' is an expanded version of '''''Head Games (2012)''''', a 2012 [[documentary film]] that examines the effects of repeated [[concussion]]s and subconcussive blows, particularly those [[Concussions in sport|associated with sports]]. It focuses on [[Concussions in American football|American football]] and hockey, but also covers [[Dementia pugilistica|boxing]], soccer, lacrosse, and professional wrestling. It covers findings that chronic [[traumatic brain injury]] is occurring in female sports. Also covered is physiological evidence of brain injury in adolescent athletes.

''Head Games'' is directed by [[Steve James (producer)|Steve James]], director of the highly acclaimed documentary, ''[[Hoop Dreams]]''.<ref>{{cite web | url=https://www.rogerebert.com/reviews/great-movie-hoop-dreams-1994 | title=Hoop Dreams | work=[[Chicago Sun-Times]] | date=July 8, 2001 | accessdate=August 9, 2018 | author=[[Ebert, Roger]]}}</ref> It is a film followup to [[Christopher Nowinski]]'s book, ''Head Games''.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://chrisnowinski.com/head-games-the-book/|title=Head Games: Football's Concussion Crisis|publisher=|accessdate=8 August 2018|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20131005013329/http://chrisnowinski.com/head-games-the-book/|archive-date=5 October 2013|url-status=dead}}</ref>

The film features interviews with Nowinski (founder of the Sports Legacy Institute), Dr. Robert Cantu (a professor of neurosurgery at [[Boston University School of Medicine]], Dr. [[Ann McKee]], and Robert Stern, who are experts on [[chronic traumatic encephalopathy]]). In addition to other medical experts, it also extensively interviews athletes, their families, and journalists.

== Critical and media reception ==
''Head Games'' was a critical success, winning Best Documentary at the 2012 [[Boston Film Festival]] and ''[[Sports Illustrated]]'' Best Sports Movie of 2012. ''Head Games'' was also an official selection for both the 2012 [[International Documentary Film Festival Amsterdam]] and the 2012 Sprout Film Festival. The films was also noted in iTunes Best of 2012 and [[Rotten Tomatoes]] Top Movies of 2012.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.rottentomatoes.com/top/bestofrt/?year=2012|title=Top 100 Movies of 2012 - Rotten Tomatoes|website=www.rottentomatoes.com|accessdate=8 August 2018}}</ref>

Roger Ebert gave the film three stars noting that "the documentary by Steve James paints a devastating picture of the long-term consequences of head injuries among pro NFL players."<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.rogerebert.com/reviews/head-games-2012|title=Head Games Movie Review & Film Summary (2012) - Roger Ebert|first=Roger|last=Ebert|website=www.rogerebert.com|accessdate=8 August 2018}}</ref> Ebert also called ''Head Games'' one of the year's "best documentaries."<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.rogerebert.com/rogers-journal/some-of-the-years-best-documentaries|title=Some of the year's best documentaries - Roger Ebert's Journal - Roger Ebert|first=Roger|last=Ebert|website=www.rogerebert.com|accessdate=8 August 2018}}</ref> The ''[[New York Times]]'' stated "''Head Games'' gains credibility and power from compassion for athletes and respect for their accomplishments. But it also tries to open the eyes of sports lovers to dangers that have too often been minimized and too seldom fully understood."<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2012/09/21/movies/head-games-a-documentary-about-athletic-injuries.html?_r=0|title='Head Games,' a Documentary About Athletic Injuries|first=A. O.|last=Scott|authorlink =A. O. Scott|newspaper =New York Times|date=20 September 2012 |accessdate=8 August 2018}}</ref> The ''[[Pittsburgh Post Gazette]]'' listed the film as one of the year's best films.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.post-gazette.com/ae/movies/2012/12/28/The-PG-s-best-movies-of-2012/stories/201212280192|title=The PG's best movies of 2012|publisher=|accessdate=8 August 2018}}</ref>

In March 2014, UK Distributor [[Dogwoof]] announced that it would be a launching doc-centric digital distribution platform entitled IF365 to help filmmakers get their work on top digital platforms. IF365 will use Dogwoof’s relationship with platforms such as [[iTunes]] and [[Netflix]] to showcase feature documentaries including ''Head Games: The Global Concussion Crisis''.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://realscreen.com/2014/03/07/dogwoof-launching-doc-centric-digital-distribution-platform/|title=Dogwoof launching doc-centric digital distribution platform|publisher=|accessdate=8 August 2018}}</ref>
