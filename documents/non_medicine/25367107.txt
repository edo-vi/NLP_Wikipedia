'''Quokkapox virus''' (QPV), also known as '''quokka poxvirus''', '''marsupial papillomavirus''', or '''marsupialpox virus''',<ref>{{Cite book
  | last = Mahy
  | first = Brian W. J.
  | title = The dictionary of virology
  | publisher = Elsevier
  | date = October 2008
  | pages = 393
  | url = https://books.google.com/books?id=ksSkuSLzXJsC
  | isbn = 978-0-12-373732-8}}</ref> is a [[DNA virus#Group I: dsDNA viruses|dsDNA virus]] that causes [[quokkapox]].  It is unclear whether this [[virus]] is its own [[species]] or a member of another species.<ref name=ICTV>{{cite web|author1=<!-- not stated -->|title=ICTV 9th Report (2011)|url=https://ictv.global/report_9th/dsDNA/poxviridae|website=ICTV|access-date=30 September 2017|location=Leiden|language=en|date=2011|quote=List of other related viruses which may be members of the subfamily Chordopoxvirinae but have not been approved as species Quokka poxvirus}}</ref>  It primarily infects the [[quokka]], which is one of only four [[Macropodidae|macropodid]] [[marsupial]]s to get pox [[lesion]]s.  The lesions can mainly be seen on the tail, and can be up to {{convert|5|cm}} in diameter.<ref>{{Cite book
