'''Cover dance''' (also known as '''dance cover''') is the act of replicating a dance [[choreography]], particularly from [[Japanese idol]] or [[Korean idol]] music acts.<ref name="Billboard2011">{{cite magazine |author=Billboard Korea Staff |date=2011-10-18 |title=A Look Inside the 'K-Pop Cover Dance' Trend |url=https://www.billboard.com/music/music-news/a-look-inside-the-k-pop-cover-dance-trend-465675/ |magazine=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]] |access-date=2023-06-29 |quote=“K-Pop Cover Dance” is a term used to describe the imitation of K-Pop artist’s dance choreography.}}</ref> Cover dances may be uploaded onto [[video hosting service|video-sharing services]] like [[YouTube]] and [[TikTok]] in which [[dancer]]s reenact the [[choreography]] of a song or [[music video]] or perform an original choreography for an existing song.<ref name=KH>{{cite web

==History==

[[File:Hare Hare Yukai 2007.jpg|thumb|214x214px|Fans performing the "Hare Hare Yukai" dance at [[Anime Expo]] 2007.]]
Cover dances first gained popularity online in Japan in March 2007, with many people posting videos of themselves performing the choreography for "[[Hare Hare Yukai]]" (colloquially referred to as the "Haruhi dance"), the ending theme song to the 2006 anime adaptation of ''[[The Melancholy of Haruhi Suzumiya]]''.<ref name="gadget 2010-01-23">{{cite news | url=https://news.livedoor.com/article/detail/4565356/ | title=『ニコニコ動画』で定番ジャンルとなった“踊ってみた”動画！　何故踊る？その心理を聞いてみた | language=ja | work=Gadget News | date=2010-01-23 | accessdate=2023-07-13 | archive-date=2010-01-30 | archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20100130060941/http://news.livedoor.com/article/detail/4565356/ | url-status=live }}</ref> This later grew to include cover dances of Japanese [[Japanese idol|idol]] singers and groups, such as [[Berryz Kobo]].<ref name="gadget 2010-01-23"/> Cover dance videos created through [[MikuMikuDance]] were also associated with the category.<ref name="ascii 2011-12-28"/> It became established as a genre known as {{nihongo|''odottemita''|踊ってみた||{{literal translation}} "I tried dancing"}} on video-sharing websites, named after the search keyword on the video-sharing website [[Niconico]].<ref name="ascii 2011-12-28">{{cite news | url=https://ascii.jp/elem/000/000/659/659162/ | title=決定版！絶対見るべきニコニコ動画2011【ダンス＆MMD編】 | trans-title=Decisive version! Videos you should watch on Nico Nico Douga in 2011 (Dance and MikuMikuDance version) | language=ja | work=[[ASCII Media Works]] | date=2011-12-28 | accessdate=2023-07-13 | archive-date=2022-08-19 | archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20220819014442/https://ascii.jp/elem/000/000/659/659162/ | url-status=live }}</ref> People who performed cover dances were known as {{nihongo|''odorite''|踊り手}}. Notable {{transl|ja|odorite}} who later became idols themselves include {{ill|Kozue Aikawa|ja|愛川こずえ}} from [[Danceroid]] and [[Dempagumi.inc]],<ref>{{cite news | url=https://wpb.shueisha.co.jp/news/technology/2016/11/25/75650/ | title=「ニコニコ動画」からアイドルデビューした愛川こずえ 「ニコニコがなかったらたぶんニートになってた（笑）」 | trans-title=Kozue Aikawa, an idol who debuted from Nico Nico Douga: "If it weren't for Niconico, I might've become a [[NEET]] (laughs)" | language=ja | work=[[Weekly Playboy]] | publisher=[[Shueisha]] | date=2016-11-25 | accessdate=2023-07-13 | archive-date=2023-07-14 | archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20230714084438/https://wpb.shueisha.co.jp/news/technology/2016/11/25/75650/ | url-status=live }}</ref> [[Beckii Cruel]],<ref>{{cite news | url=https://www.oricon.co.jp/news/72162/full/ | title=“可愛いにもほどがある!”英ネットアイドルのベッキー・クルーエルが初のテレビ出演! | trans-title="She has so much cuteness!"
