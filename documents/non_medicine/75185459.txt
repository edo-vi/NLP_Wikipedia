'''Interdigitation''' is the interlinking of biological components that resembles the fingers of two hands being locked together. It can be a naturally occurring or man-made state. 

==Examples==
Naturally occurring interdigitation includes [[skull suture]]s that develop during periods of brain growth, and which remain thin and straight, and later develop complex fractal interdigitations that provide interlocking strength.<ref>{{Cite journal|title=Mechanism of skull suture maintenance and interdigitation|first1=Takashi|last1=Miura|first2=Chad A|last2=Perlyn|first3=Masato|last3=Kinboshi|first4=Naomichi|last4=Ogihara|first5=Mikiko|last5=Kobayashi-Miura|first6=Gillian M|last6=Morriss-Kay|first7=Kohei|last7=Shiota|date=December 30, 2009|journal=Journal of Anatomy|volume=215|issue=6|pages=642–655|doi=10.1111/j.1469-7580.2009.01148.x|pmid=19811566|pmc=2796787}}</ref> A layer of the retina where photoreception occurs is called the interdigitation zone.<ref>{{Cite journal|title=Early-stage macular telangiectasia type 2 vascular abnormalities are associated with interdigitation zone disruption|first1=Janice X.|last1=Ong|first2=Roya|last2=Zandi|first3=Amani A.|last3=Fawzi|date=November 12, 2021|journal=PLOS ONE|volume=16|issue=11|pages=e0259811|doi=10.1371/journal.pone.0259811|pmid=34767582|pmc=8589180 |bibcode=2021PLoSO..1659811O |doi-access=free }}</ref> [[Adhesion]] or diffusive bonding occurs when sections of polymer chains from one surface interdigitate with those of an adjacent surface. In the [[dermis]], [[dermal papillae]] (DP) (singular papilla, diminutive of Latin papula, 'pimple') are small, nipple-like extensions  of the dermis into the [[epidermis]], also known as interdigitations. The [[distal convoluted tubule]] (DCT), a portion of [[kidney]] [[nephron]], can be recognized by several distinct features, including lateral membrane interdigitations with neighboring cells.<ref>{{Cite journal|url=http://journals.sagepub.com/doi/10.1177/019262339802600101|title=Normal Ultrastructure of the Kidney and Lower Urinary Tract|first=Jill W.|last=Verlander|date=January 30, 1998|journal=Toxicologic Pathology|volume=26|issue=1|pages=1–17|via=CrossRef|doi=10.1177/019262339802600101|pmid=9502381 |s2cid=38448713 }}</ref>

Some hypotheses contend that [[crown shyness]], the interdigitation of canopy branches, leads to "reciprocal pruning" of adjacent trees.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.iflscience.com/crown-shyness-why-some-trees-avoid-touching-leaves-creating-a-fractured-canopy-59993|title=Crown Shyness: Why Some Trees Avoid Touching Leaves, Creating A Fractured Canopy|date=June 15, 2021|website=IFLScience}}</ref>

Interdigitation is also found in biological research. Interdigitation fusion is a method of preparing calcium- and phosphate-loaded liposomes.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.sciencedirect.com/topics/engineering/interdigitation|title=Interdigitation - an overview &#124; ScienceDirect Topics|website=www.sciencedirect.com}}</ref> Drugs inserted in the bilayer biomembrane may influence the lateral organization of the lipid membrane, with interdigitation of the membrane to fill volume voids.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.sciencedirect.com/bookseries/advances-in-biomembranes-and-lipid-self-assembly|title=Advances in Biomembranes and Lipid Self-Assembly &#124; Book series &#124; ScienceDirect.com by Elsevier|website=www.sciencedirect.com}}</ref> A similar interdigitation process involves investigating [[dissipative particle dynamics]] (DPD) simulations by adding alcohol molecules to the bilayers of double-tail lipids.<ref>{{Cite journal|title=Simulating Induced Interdigitation in Membranes|first1=Marieke|last1=Kranenburg|first2=Martin|last2=Vlaar|first3=Berend|last3=Smit|date=September 30, 2004|journal=Biophysical Journal|volume=87|issue=3|pages=1596–1605|doi=10.1529/biophysj.104.045005|pmid=15345539|pmc=1304565|bibcode=2004BpJ....87.1596K }}</ref> Pressure-induced interdigitation is used to study hydrostatic pressure of bicellular dispersions containing [[anionic]] lipids.<ref>{{Cite journal|url=https://pubs.acs.org/doi/10.1021/la4035694|title=Response to Hydrostatic Pressure of Bicellar Dispersions Containing an Anionic Lipid: Pressure-Induced Interdigitation|first1=Ashkan|last1=Rahmani|first2=Collin|last2=Knight|first3=Michael R.|last3=Morrow|date=November 5, 2013|journal=Langmuir|volume=29|issue=44|pages=13481–13490|via=CrossRef|doi=10.1021/la4035694|pmid=24116385 }}</ref>
