The '''2018 Équateur province Ebola outbreak''' occurred in the north-west of the [[Democratic Republic of the Congo]] (DRC) from May to July 2018. It was contained entirely within [[Province of Équateur|Équateur province]], and was the first time that vaccination with the [[rVSV-ZEBOV]] Ebola vaccine had been attempted in the early stages of an Ebola outbreak,<ref name=Cohen_Science_ /> with a total of 3,481 people vaccinated.<ref name="who17">{{Cite news|url=https://reliefweb.int/report/democratic-republic-congo/democratic-republic-congo-ebola-virus-disease-external-situation-14|title=Democratic Republic of Congo: Ebola Virus Disease - External Situation Report 17: Declaration of End of Ebola Virus Disease Outbreak|work=ReliefWeb|access-date=26 July 2018|language=en}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.vox.com/science-and-health/2018/5/16/17356464/ebola-virus-vaccine|title=We finally have an Ebola vaccine. And we're using it in an outbreak|website=Vox|access-date=10 July 2018|date=2018-05-16}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.washingtonexaminer.com/policy/healthcare/health-officials-contain-ebolas-spread-in-the-congo|title=Health officials contain Ebola's spread in the Congo|date=6 July 2018|website=Washington Examiner|language=en|access-date=10 July 2018}}</ref> It was the ninth recorded Ebola outbreak in the DRC.<ref name=who17/>

The outbreak began on 8 May 2018, when it was reported that 17 people were suspected of having died from EVD near the town of [[Bikoro]] in the [[Province of Équateur]].<ref name="gua">{{cite news|url=https://www.theguardian.com/world/2018/may/08/drc-confirms-two-cases-of-ebola-in-north-western-town|title=DRC: at least 17 people dead in confirmed Ebola outbreak|date=8 May 2018|work=The Guardian|access-date=9 May 2018|agency=[[Reuters]]|language=en}}</ref> The [[World Health Organization]] declared the outbreak after two people were confirmed as having the disease.<ref name="who">{{cite news|url=https://www.who.int/news-room/detail/08-05-2018-new-ebola-outbreak-declared-in-democratic-republic-of-the-congo|title=New Ebola outbreak declared in Democratic Republic of the Congo|date=8 May 2018|work=World Health Organization|access-date=9 May 2018}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.cbsnews.com/news/congo-ebola-cases-confirmed-new-outbreak-2018-08-05/|title=New Ebola outbreak hits Africa as 2 cases confirmed in Congo|date=8 May 2018|work=CBS News|access-date=9 May 2018|agency=[[Associated Press]]|language=en}}</ref> On 17 May, the virus was confirmed to have spread to the [[inland port]] city of [[Mbandaka]], causing the WHO to raise its assessment of the national risk level to "very high",<ref name=who-2018-05-17/><ref name=":0" /> but not yet to constitute an [[Public Health Emergency of International Concern|international public health emergency]].<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/world-africa-44164027|title=Ebola in DR Congo 'not yet global crisis'|date=18 May 2018|work=BBC News|access-date=19 May 2018|language=en-GB}}</ref> The WHO declared the outbreak over on 24 July 2018, with 33 confirmed deaths.<ref name="who12"/><ref name="reliefweb23july">{{cite web |title=Media Advisory: Expected end of Ebola outbreak |url=https://reliefweb.int/report/democratic-republic-congo/media-advisory-expected-end-ebola-outbreak |website=ReliefWeb |date=23 July 2018 |access-date=23 July 2018 |language=en}}</ref><ref name=WHO_epidemic_ends>{{cite web |title=Ebola outbreak in DRC ends: WHO calls for international efforts to stop other deadly outbreaks in the country |url=https://www.who.int/news-room/detail/24-07-2018-ebola-outbreak-in-drc-ends-who-calls-for-international-efforts-to-stop-other-deadly-outbreaks-in-the-country |website=World Health Organization |access-date=24 July 2018}}</ref>

Subsequent to the end of this outbreak, the [[Kivu Ebola epidemic]] commenced in the eastern region of the country on 1 August 2018;<ref name="DRC_Ebola10">{{cite news |title=Congo declares new Ebola outbreak in eastern province |url=https://www.reuters.com/article/us-health-ebola/congo-declares-new-ebola-outbreak-in-eastern-province-idUSKBN1KM59B?il=0 |newspaper=Reuters |access-date=1 August 2018|date=August 2018 }}</ref> it was declared over on 25 June 2020 with 2,280 deaths recorded.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.who.int/news/item/25-06-2020-10th-ebola-outbreak-in-the-democratic-republic-of-the-congo-declared-over-vigilance-against-flare-ups-and-support-for-survivors-must-continue |title=10th Ebola outbreak in the Democratic Republic of the Congo declared over; vigilance against flare-ups and support for survivors must continue |publisher=WHO |date=25 June 2020 |access-date=4 November 2020}}</ref> A further separate outbreak in the [[Province of Équateur]] was announced on 1 June 2020 by the Congolese health ministry, described as the eleventh Ebola outbreak since records began.<ref>{{cite web|title=New outbreak declared in Equateur province|url=https://www.msf.org/drc-ebola-outbreak-crisis-update|website=Médecins Sans Frontières|access-date=7 June 2020|date=4 June 2020}}</ref> This eleventh outbreak was declared over as of 18 November following no reported cases for 42 days,<ref name="2020over1">{{Cite web|date=2020-11-18|title=For the first time since 2018, there is no active Ebola outbreak in the DRC|url=http://cd.usembassy.gov/for-the-first-time-since-2018-there-is-no-active-ebola-outbreak-in-the-drc/|access-date=2020-11-18|website=U.S. Embassy in the Democratic Republic of the Congo|language=en-US}}</ref> having caused 130 cases and 55 deaths.<ref name="2020over2">{{cite web |title=UNICEF welcomes end of Ebola outbreak in the Equateur Province of the DRC |url=https://www.unicef.org/press-releases/unicef-welcomes-end-ebola-outbreak-equateur-province-drc |website=www.unicef.org |access-date=18 November 2020 |language=en}}</ref>

After his funeral, eleven family members became ill, and seven of them died. All of the seven deceased had attended the man's funeral or cared for him while he was sick.<ref name = "patient zero"/> The identification of this individual as the index case has not yet been confirmed.<ref name="ESR4" />

Équateur province's Provincial Health Division reported 21 cases with symptoms consistent with [[Ebola virus disease]], of whom 17 had died, on 3 May 2018.<ref name="ESR3" /> Of these, eight cases were subsequently  shown not to have been Ebola-related.<ref name="who05" /> The outbreak was declared on 8 May after samples from two of five patients in Bikoro tested positive for the [[Zaire ebolavirus|Zaire strain]] of the Ebola virus.<ref name="who" /><ref name="ESR3" /><ref name=gua/>

On 10 May, the World Health Organization (WHO) stated that the Democratic Republic of the Congo had a total of 32 cases of EVD,<ref name=who1/> and a further two suspected cases were announced on the following day, bringing the total cases to 34, all located in the Bikoro area of DRC.<ref name="who2">{{cite web|title=WHO and partners working with national health authorities to contain new Ebola outbreak in the Democratic Republic of the Congo|url=https://www.who.int/news-room/detail/11-05-2018-who-and-partners-working-with-national-health-authorities-to-contain-new-ebola-outbreak-in-the-democratic-republic-of-the-congo|website=World Health Organization|access-date=11 May 2018}}</ref>

===Spread to Mbandaka===
In the eight previous Ebola outbreaks in DRC since 1976, the virus had never before reached a major city. In May 2018, for the first time, four cases were confirmed in the city of Mbandaka.<ref name="dw.com">{{cite news |last1=Hilse |first1=Gwendolin |title=DR Congo declares Ebola outbreak over |url=https://www.dw.com/en/dr-congo-declares-ebola-outbreak-over/a-44784301 |access-date=25 July 2018 |publisher=DW |date=24 July 2018}}</ref><ref name="who05" />

On 14 May, suspected cases were reported in the [[Iboko]] and [[Wangata]] areas in [[Province of Équateur|Équateur province]], in addition to Bikoro. The WHO reported on 17 May 2018 that the first case of this outbreak in an urban area<ref name = "patient zero"/> had been confirmed in the Wangata district of Mbandaka city, the capital of Équateur province, about 100 miles north of Bikoro.<ref name= "patient zero"/><ref name=ESR2 /><ref name=who-2018-05-17>{{Cite news|url=https://www.who.int/news-room/detail/17-05-2018-who-concerned-as-one-ebola-case-confirmed-in-urban-area-of-democratic-republic-of-the-congo|title=WHO concerned as one Ebola case confirmed in urban area of Democratic Republic of the Congo|work=World Health Organization|access-date=17 May 2018|language=en-US}}</ref>  Mbandaka is a busy, densely populated port on the [[Congo River]] with a population of 1.2 million,<ref name="WHO_IHR1">{{cite web|url=https://www.who.int/news-room/detail/18-05-2018-statement-on-the-1st-meeting-of-the-ihr-emergency-committee-regarding-the-ebola-outbreak-in-2018|title=Statement on the 1st meeting of the IHR Emergency Committee regarding the Ebola outbreak in 2018|website=World Health Organization|access-date=18 May 2018}}</ref> leading to a high risk of contagion.<ref name="ESR3" /><ref name= "patient zero"/> The following day, the WHO raised the health risk in DRC to "very high" due to the presence of the virus in an urban area.<ref name=":0">{{cite news|url=https://www.cnn.com/2018/05/18/health/ebola-outbreak-drc-intl/index.html|title=WHO raises Ebola health risk to 'very high' in DRC|last1=McKirdy|first1=Euan|date=18 May 2018|work=CNN|access-date=18 May 2018|last2=Senthilingam|first2=Meera}}</ref>

The DRC government was particularly concerned about the virus spreading by boat transport along the Congo between Mbandaka and the capital, [[Kinshasa]].<ref name=Telegraph_23_May_2018>{{cite news |url=https://www.telegraph.co.uk/news/0/congo-knife-edge-number-cases-ebola-continues-rise/ |title=Congo at a knife edge as number of cases of Ebola continues to rise |author=Anne Gulland |author2=Louise Dewast |newspaper=[[The Daily Telegraph]] |date=23 May 2018 |access-date=23 May 2018 }}</ref> The WHO also considered that there was a high risk of the outbreak spreading to nine other countries in the region,<ref name="ESR3" /> including the bordering [[Republic of the Congo]] and [[Central African Republic]].<ref name=Telegraph_23_May_2018 /><ref name=WHO_IHR1 />

{{As of |2018|5|23}}, the focus of the outbreak was split between Bikoro and Iboko; Iboko had 55% of the confirmed cases of EVD<ref name="who05" /> and Bikoro had 81.5% of the fatalities.<ref name="ESR4" /> The cases in the Bikoro health zone were located in [[Ikoko Impenge]] (12), Bikoro (6), [[Momboyo]] (1) and Moheli (1); those in the Iboko health zone were located in Itipo (13), Mpangi (2), Wenga (1) and Loongo (1).<ref name="who05" />

According to the fifth situation report released by the WHO, the [[case fatality rate]] (CFR) was 42.3%.<ref name="who05" /> Demographics had been reported for 44 cases as of 22 May; there were 26 cases of EVD in men and 18 in women; 7 cases were in children 14 years and under, and 9 were in those over 60 years.<ref name="who05" />  By 23 May, there had been 5 reported cases in health-care workers, including two who died.<ref name="who05" /> [[Contact tracing]] was being employed to identify contacts with infected individuals.<ref name="ESR4" /><ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.reuters.com/article/us-ebola-health-congo/congo-u-n-deploy-specialists-to-tackle-ebola-epidemic-idUSKCN1IE0J9|title=Congo, U.N. deploy specialists to tackle Ebola epidemic|publisher=Reuters|date=13 May 2018|access-date=13 May 2018|language=en-US}}</ref> On 29 May, it was reported that 800 contacts had been identified in the city of Mbandaka;<ref>{{cite web |author=Meera Senthilingam |title=Congo Ebola outbreak: 5 experimental drugs to be tried |url=https://www.cnn.com/2018/05/29/health/ebola-experimental-vaccine-treatment-congo-intl/index.html |website=CNN |date=29 May 2018 |access-date=31 May 2018}}</ref> the next day it was reported that 500 people in the city had been vaccinated.<ref>{{cite web |title=Nearly 700 get Ebola vaccine in Congo; more cases possible |url=http://www.foxnews.com/health/2018/06/01/nearly-700-get-ebola-vaccine-in-congo-more-cases-possible.html |website=Fox News |access-date=1 June 2018 |date=1 June 2018}}</ref>

On 29 May, the WHO indicated that nine neighbouring countries had been alerted for being at high risk of spread of EVD,<ref>{{cite web |title=Ebola virus disease – Democratic Republic of the Congo |url=https://www.who.int/csr/don/30-may-2018-ebola-drc/en/ |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180602170724/http://www.who.int/csr/don/30-may-2018-ebola-drc/en/ |url-status=dead |archive-date=2 June 2018 |website=World Health Organization |access-date=3 June 2018}}</ref> On 4 June, it was reported that Angola had closed its border with the DRC due to the outbreak.<ref>{{cite web |title=Ebola : Le gouvernement de Malanje (Angola) ferme la frontière avec la RDC — Actualite.CD |url=https://actualite.cd/2018/06/04/ebola-le-gouvernement-de-malanje-angola-ferme-la-frontiere-avec-la-rdc/ |website=Actualite.CD |access-date=4 June 2018 |language=fr-FR |date=4 June 2018}}</ref>

===End of the outbreak===
This outbreak in the Democratic Republic of the Congo was declared over on 24 July 2018 after 42 days passed without any new confirmed cases.<ref name="voa">{{cite web |last1=Schlein |first1=Lisa |title=Official End to Congo Ebola Outbreak Seen Near |url=https://www.voanews.com/a/congo-ebola-outbreak-expected-to-end-next-week/4486095.html |website=VOA |date=17 July 2018 |access-date=21 July 2018 |language=en}}</ref><ref name=reliefweb23july/><ref name="dw.com"/> Although it was noted that a new outbreak occurred only one week later in the eastern region of [[Kivu]];   and it has  been established that they are not linked.<ref name="DRC_Ebola10"/>

==Containment challenges==
The Bikoro area had three hospitals, but the area's health services were described by WHO as predominantly having "limited functionality";<ref name="ESR3" /> they received supplies from international bodies but experienced frequent shortages. More than half of the Bikoro area cases were in [[Ikoko-Impenge]], a village not connected to the road system.<ref name="ESR3" /><ref name="who05" /> Bikoro lies in dense [[rain forest]],<ref name= "patient zero"/> and the area's remoteness and inadequate infrastructure hindered treatment of EVD patients, as well as surveillance and vaccination efforts.<ref name=WHO_IHR1 />

Adherence was another challenge: on 20–21 May, three individuals with EVD in an isolation ward of a treatment center in [[Mbandaka]] fled; two later died after attending a prayer meeting, at which they may have exposed 50 other attendees to the virus.<ref>{{cite web|title=Two infected Ebola patients flee treatment center in Congo|url=https://www.cbsnews.com/news/ebola-outbreak-2018-two-infected-patients-flee-treatment-center-in-congo/|website=CBS news|date=23 May 2018 |access-date=23 May 2018|language=en}}</ref><ref name=BBC_23_May_2018>{{cite news |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/world-africa-44229346 |title=Ebola outbreak in DR Congo: Patients 'taken to church' |publisher=BBC |date=23 May 2018 |access-date=23 May 2018}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|last1=Nebehay|first1=Stephanie|title=Infection alert after dying Ebola patients taken to Congo prayer…|url=https://www.reuters.com/article/us-health-ebola-congo/two-congo-ebola-patients-attended-church-with-50-people-before-dying-msf-idUSKCN1IP1PT|newspaper=Reuters|access-date=24 May 2018|date=2018-05-24}}</ref><ref name=ProMED_24_May_2018 />
[[Bushmeat]] was believed to be one vector of infection, but bushmeat vendors at the Mbandaka market told reporters that they did not believe Ebola was real or serious.<ref>{{cite web |title=At the center of Ebola in the Congo, worry and indifference coexist |author=Louise Dewast |website=[[ABC News]] |date=31 May 2018 |url=https://abcnews.go.com/amp/International/center-ebola-congo-worry-indifference-coexist/story%3fid=55531944 |access-date=3 June 2018 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180601204000/https://abcnews.go.com/amp/International/center-ebola-congo-worry-indifference-coexist/story?id=55531944 |archive-date=1 June 2018 |url-status=dead }}</ref> Hostility towards health workers trying to offer medical assistance was also reported.<ref name=Ross_25_May_2018>{{cite news |url=https://www.reuters.com/article/us-health-ebola-congo-escape/fear-and-suspicion-hinder-congo-medics-in-ebola-battle-idUSKCN1IQ1R2 |title=Fear and suspicion hinder Congo medics in Ebola battle |author=Aaron Ross |publisher=Reuters |date=25 May 2018 |access-date=28 May 2018 }}</ref> On 29 May, the WHO forecast that there would be 100–300 cases by the end of July.<ref>{{cite news|last1=Miles|first1=Tom|title=WHO's Congo Ebola plan assumes 100-300 cases over three months|url=https://www.reuters.com/article/us-health-ebola/whos-congo-ebola-plan-assumes-100-300-cases-over-three-months-idUSKCN1IU0XC|newspaper=Reuters|access-date=29 May 2018|date=2018-05-29}}</ref>

==Virology==
{{further|Ebola virus}}
[[File:Ebola virus virion.jpg|thumb|180 px|Ebola virus virion]]
[[Ebola virus|Zaire ebolavirus]], which was identified in this outbreak,<ref name="ESR3" /> is included in genus ''[[Ebolavirus]]'', family ''[[Filoviridae]]'',<ref>{{cite journal |last1=Mühlberger |first1=Elke |title=Filovirus replication and transcription |journal=Future Virology |date=March 2007 |volume=2 |issue=2 |pages=205–215 |doi=10.2217/17460794.2.2.205 |pmid=24093048 |pmc=3787895 }}</ref>

The virus was named for the [[Ebola River]], which runs as a tributary of the [[Congo River]] in the Democratic Republic of the Congo; the [[Zaire ebolavirus|Zaire strain]] was first identified in 1976 in [[Yambuku]].<ref>{{cite web|title=How Ebola got its name|url=https://www.spectator.co.uk/2014/10/how-ebola-got-its-name/|website=The Spectator|access-date=20 May 2018|date=25 October 2014}}</ref><ref>{{cite book|last1=Newman|first1=Patricia|title=Ebola: Fears and Facts|date=2015|publisher=Lerner Publishing Group|isbn=9781467792400|page=4|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=fmT5CAAAQBAJ&q=congo+river+ebola+river&pg=PA4|access-date=20 May 2018|language=en}}</ref>

==Response==
[[Médecins Sans Frontières]] (MSF) established treatment centers in Bikoro, Ikoko and Wangata.<ref name="who05" /> WHO sent an expert team to Bikoro on 8 May,<ref name=who/> and on 13 May, WHO Director-General [[Tedros Adhanom Ghebreyesus]] visited the town.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.who.int/news-room/detail/13-05-2018-who-director-general-visits-ebola-affected-areas-in-dr-congo|title=WHO Director General visits Ebola-affected areas in DR Congo|work=World Health Organization|access-date=14 May 2018|language=en-US}}</ref> On 18 May, the WHO IHR committee met and decided against declaring a [[Public Health Emergency of International Concern]].<ref name="WHO_IHR1" /> {{As of |2018|5|24}}, WHO had sent 138 technical personnel to the three affected areas; the Red Cross  sent more than 150 people, and [[UNICEF]] personnel were also active.<ref name="who05" /> Other international agencies sending teams included the UK Public Health Rapid Support Team<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.gov.uk/government/news/ebola-outbreak-uk-public-health-rapid-support-team-deploys-to-drc|title=Ebola outbreak: UK Public Health Rapid Support Team deploys to DRC|work=GOV.UK|access-date=2018-05-21|language=en}}</ref> and the Africa Centres for Disease Control and Prevention.<ref name=Reuters_22_May_2018>{{cite news |url=https://www.reuters.com/article/us-health-ebola/two-more-die-of-ebola-in-congo-seven-new-cases-confirmed-idUSKCN1IN0WP |title=Two more die of Ebola in Congo; seven new cases confirmed |author=Patient Ligodi |publisher=Reuters |date=22 May 2018 |access-date=22 May 2018}}</ref> The [[Wellcome Trust]]  donated £2 million towards the DRC outbreak.<ref>{{cite web|title=Wellcome pledges £2m after new Ebola outbreak confirmed {{!}} Wellcome|url=https://wellcome.ac.uk/news/wellcome-pledges-£2m-after-new-ebola-outbreak-confirmed|website=wellcome.ac.uk|access-date=11 May 2018|language=en}}</ref> [[Merck & Co.|Merck]] donated its experimental vaccine and the [[GAVI]] international vaccination alliance helped to support vaccination operations.<ref name=WHO_vacc_21_May_2018 /> Several tons of supplies were shipped to the DRC, including protection and disinfection kits and palliative drugs.<ref name = "patient zero"/> After the virus spread to the city of Mbandaka, DRC health minister [[Oly Ilunga Kalenga]] announced that healthcare would be provided free for those affected.<ref name=Guardian_18_May_2018>{{cite news |url=https://www.theguardian.com/world/2018/may/18/more-ebola-cases-confirmed-in-congolese-city-of-mbandaka |title=Ebola: two more cases confirmed in Mbandaka in DRC |author=Jason Burke |newspaper=[[The Guardian]] |date=18 May 2018 |access-date=22 May 2018}}</ref><ref name=WashingtonPost_21_May_2018>{{cite news |url=https://www.washingtonpost.com/world/africa/nurse-dead-in-congo-as-ebola-vaccination-campaign-starts/2018/05/21/1b71082e-5ce7-11e8-b656-236c6214ef01_story.html |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190114041819/https://www.washingtonpost.com/world/africa/nurse-dead-in-congo-as-ebola-vaccination-campaign-starts/2018/05/21/1b71082e-5ce7-11e8-b656-236c6214ef01_story.html |url-status=dead |archive-date=14 January 2019 |author=Saleh Mwanamilongo |title=Congo Ebola vaccination campaign begins with health workers |newspaper=[[The Washington Post]] |date=21 May 2018 |access-date=22 May 2018 }}</ref>

US President [[Donald Trump]] has advocated rescinding Ebola funding and most financing for State Department emergency responses. National Security Advisor [[John Bolton]] removed the National Security Council's health security chief on the day that the Ebola outbreak was declared, shutting down the entire epidemic prevention office.<ref name="fp">{{cite news |title=Trump Is in a Coma on Public Health |url=https://foreignpolicy.com/2018/05/21/trump-is-in-a-coma-on-public-health/|access-date=8 June 2018 |work=Foreign Policy |language=en}}</ref> The U.S. [[Centers for Disease Control and Prevention]] says "about five" of its staff advise the Congolese government. CDC presence appears to be smaller than West African countries'. The U.S. [[Agency for International Development]] promised $1 million to WHO for Ebola efforts. Germany has promised $5.8 million.<ref name=fp/>

===Surveillance===
Surveillance of travelers at Mbandaka's port and airport was performed.<ref name="ESR3" /> The DRC Ministry of Public Health identified 115 areas where movement of people increased the risk of virus transmission, including 83 river ports, nine airports and seven bus stations, as well as 16 markets.<ref name="ESR4" /> [[José Makila]], the DRC minister of transport,  stated that the [[Navy of the Democratic Republic of the Congo|Navy]] would be used to surveil river traffic on the Congo.<ref name=Telegraph_23_May_2018 /> On 10 May, the [[Nigerian Ministry of Health]] reported it would start screening at its borders,<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.cnn.com/2018/05/10/africa/nigeria-ebola-screening/index.html|title=Ebola: Nigeria begins screening of travelers from high-risk countries|last=Adebayo|first=Bukola|work=CNN|access-date=11 May 2018|date=10 May 2018}}</ref> and on 18 May 2018, a total of 20 countries had instituted screening of travelers coming from the DRC.<ref name="ESR3" /> WHO sent teams to 8/9 of the neighboring countries to assess their capability to deal with EVD spread and facilitate their surveillance.<ref name="who05" /> The DRC Ministry of Public Health worked with surveyors and cartographers from [[UCLA]] and OpenStreetMap DRC to improve mapping of the affected area.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.theatlantic.com/health/archive/2018/05/most-maps-of-the-new-ebola-outbreak-are-wrong/560777/ |title=Most Maps of the New Ebola Outbreak Are Wrong |author=Ed Yong |newspaper=[[The Atlantic]] |date=21 May 2018 |access-date=21 May 2018 }}</ref> A laboratory commenced operations in Bikoro on 16 May, enabling local testing of patient samples for Ebola virus.<ref name="ESR4" />

Burials were organized by MSF and the [[Red Cross of the Democratic Republic of the Congo]] to minimize the risk of transmission.<ref name="ESR3" /> The [[United Nations Radio]]  broadcast EVD awareness information, and posters and leaflets were prepared and distributed.<ref name="ESR3" /> UNICEF warned 143 churches across Mbandaka of the risks of prayer meetings.<ref name="who05" />

==Treatment==
===Ring vaccination with rVSV-ZEBOV===
{{further|Ebola vaccine}}
[[File:RVSV-ZEBOV.png|70 px|left|thumb|ZEBOV]]
[[File:Ebola and Contact Tracing.webm|thumb|Ebola and contact tracing]]
Health authorities including DRC's [[Ministry of Public Health (Democratic Republic of the Congo)|Ministry of Public Health]] used [[Recombinant virus|recombinant]] [[vesicular stomatitis virus]]–[[Zaire Ebola virus]] ([[rVSV-ZEBOV vaccine|rVSV-ZEBOV]]) vaccine – a recently developed experimental [[Ebola vaccine]], produced by [[Merck & Co.|Merck]] – to try to suppress the outbreak. This [[live-attenuated vaccine]] expresses the surface [[glycoprotein]] of the Kikwit 1995 strain of [[Zaire ebolavirus]] in a recombinant [[vesicular stomatitis virus]] [[Vector (molecular biology)|vector]].<ref name=Regule_NEJM_2017>{{cite journal |author=J. A. Regule |display-authors=et al |title=A Recombinant Vesicular Stomatitis Virus Ebola Vaccine |journal=[[New England Journal of Medicine]] |year=2017 |volume=376 |issue=4 |pages=330–41 |doi=10.1056/NEJMoa1414216 |pmid=25830322 |pmc=5408576 }}</ref>  rVSV-ZEBOV was trialed in [[Guinea]] and [[Sierra Leone]] during the [[West African Ebola virus epidemic|West African epidemic]] of 2013–16, with 5837 people receiving the vaccine; the trial authors concluded that rVSV-ZEBOV provided "substantial protection" against EVD,<ref>{{cite journal |author=Ana Maria Henao-Restrepo |display-authors=et al |title=Efficacy and effectiveness of an rVSV-vectored vaccine in preventing Ebola virus disease: final results from the Guinea ring vaccination, open-label, cluster-randomised trial (Ebola Ça Suffit!) |journal=[[The Lancet|Lancet]] |year=2017 |volume=389 |issue=10068 |pages=505–18 |doi=10.1016/S0140-6736(16)32621-6|pmid=28017403 |pmc=5364328 }}</ref> but subsequent commentators have questioned the degree of protection obtained<ref>{{cite journal |author=Wolfram G Metzger |author2=Sarai Vivas-Martínez |title=Questionable efficacy of the rVSV-ZEBOV Ebola vaccine |journal=[[The Lancet|Lancet]] |volume=391 |issue=10125 |page=1021 |doi=10.1016/S0140-6736(18)30560-9 |pmid=29565013 |year=2018 |doi-access=free }}</ref> and the degree of long-term protection conferred is unknown.<ref>{{cite journal |last1=Medaglini |first1=Donata |last2=Santoro |first2=Francesco |last3=Siegrist |first3=Claire-Anne |title=Correlates of vaccine-induced protective immunity against Ebola virus disease |journal=Seminars in Immunology |date=October 2018 |volume=39 |pages=65–72 |doi=10.1016/j.smim.2018.07.003 |pmid=30041831 |url=https://archive-ouverte.unige.ch/unige:124313 |doi-access=free |hdl=11365/1064164 |hdl-access=free }}</ref> As the vaccine had not been approved by any regulatory authority, it was used in DRC under a [[compassionate use]] trial protocol.<ref name=Cohen_Science_ />

A [[ring vaccination]] strategy was used, which involves vaccinating only those most likely to be infected: direct contacts of infected individuals, and contacts of those contacts.<ref name="patient zero"/><ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2018/05/11/health/ebola-africa-vaccine.html|title=Ebola Erupts Again in Africa, Only Now There's a Vaccine|date=11 May 2018|work=The New York Times|access-date=11 May 2018|language=en-US|issn=0362-4331}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.npr.org/sections/goatsandsoda/2018/05/15/611267872/can-the-new-ebola-vaccine-stop-the-latest-outbreak|title=Can The New Ebola Vaccine Stop The Latest Outbreak?|last=Aizenman|first=Nurith|date=15 May 2018|work=NPR.org|access-date=16 May 2018|language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2018/05/planning-worst-case-scenario-drc-ebola-outbreak-180511153458973.html|title=WHO planning for 'worst case scenario' over DRC Ebola outbreak|website=www.aljazeera.com|access-date=14 May 2018}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|title=19 dead in latest Congo Ebola outbreak: WHO|url=https://www.reuters.com/video/2018/05/14/19-dead-in-latest-congo-ebola-outbreak-w?videoId=426863714&videoChannel=118261|access-date=14 May 2018|date=14 May 2018|work=Reuters}}</ref> Other groups targeted included health workers, laboratory personnel, surveillance workers and people involved with burials.<ref name=WHO_vacc_21_May_2018 /><ref name=BBC_21_May_2018>{{cite news |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/world-africa-44194065 |title=Ebola outbreak: Experimental vaccinations to begin in DR Congo |publisher=BBC |date=21 May 2018 |access-date=21 May 2018 }}</ref> People who were vaccinated were followed up for 84 days to assess whether they were protected from infection and to monitor any adverse events.<ref name=Cohen_Science_ /> A total of 4,320 doses of the rVSV-ZEBOV vaccine were delivered to DRC's capital [[Kinshasa]] by WHO on 16 May, and a further 3,240 doses arrived three days later;<ref name="ESR4" /> with another 8,000 doses to be made available.<ref name=DON_23_May_2018>{{cite web |title=Ebola virus disease – Democratic Republic of the Congo, Disease outbreak news  |date=23 May 2018 |publisher=WHO |url=https://www.who.int/csr/don/23-may-2018-ebola-drc/en/|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180523190302/http://www.who.int/csr/don/23-may-2018-ebola-drc/en/|url-status=dead|archive-date=23 May 2018}}</ref> The vaccine must be transported and stored at between −60 and −80&nbsp;°C.<ref name=WHO_vacc_21_May_2018 /> A [[cold chain]] was established in Kinshasa by 18 May and has been extended to Mbandaka. WHO planned to concentrate on vaccinating three sets of contacts of confirmed EVD cases, two in Bikoro and one in Mbandaka.<ref name="ESR3" />
[[File:Containing-Ebola-at-the-Source-with-Ring-Vaccination-pntd.0005093.s003.ogv|150px|thumb|left|Ring vaccination strategy]]
Vaccination started on 21 May among health workers in Mbandaka,<ref name=WashingtonPost_21_May_2018 /> with 7,560 vaccine doses ready for immediate use, according to WHO.<ref name="ESR4" /><ref name=WHO_vacc_21_May_2018>{{cite web|title=WHO supports Ebola vaccination of high risk populations in the Democratic Republic of the Congo|url=https://www.who.int/news-room/detail/21-05-2018-who-supports-ebola-vaccination-of-high-risk-populations-in-the-democratic-republic-of-the-congo|website=World Health Organization|access-date=21 May 2018}}</ref> The DRC health minister [[Oly Ilunga Kalenga]] stated that vaccination of health workers and Ebola case contacts in the Wangata and [[Bolenge]] areas of Mbandaka would take five days, after which vaccination would start in Bikoro and Iboko.<ref name=WashingtonPost_21_May_2018 /> {{As of |2018|5|24}}, 154 people in Mbandaka had been vaccinated, and preparations were started for vaccinating in Bikoro and Iboko.<ref name="who05" /> Up to 1,000 people were expected to have been vaccinated by 26 May, according to WHO.<ref name="ESR4" /> It was the first time that vaccination had been attempted in the early stages of an Ebola outbreak.<ref name=Cohen_Science_ />

The rVSV-ZEBOV vaccine proved effective for the strain of the Ebola virus in this outbreak, having via ring vaccination protected some 3,481 individuals.<ref name=who17/>

===Experimental therapeutic agents===

{{further|Ebola virus disease treatment research}}
[[File:Favipiravir.svg|thumb|90 px|[[Chemical formula]] of [[favipiravir]]]]
Health officials considered trialing experimental treatments, including the antiviral agents [[favipiravir]] and [[remdesivir|GS-5734]], and the antibody [[ZMapp]]. All three agents were given to patients during the West African epidemic, but none has yet been proved to be effective.<ref name=Nature_18_May_2018>{{cite journal |author=Erika Check Hayden |title=Experimental drugs poised for use in Ebola outbreak |journal=[[Nature (journal)|Nature]] |volume=557 |issue=7706 |pages=475–476 |date=18 May 2018 |doi=10.1038/d41586-018-05205-x|pmid=29789732 |bibcode=2018Natur.557..475C |doi-access=free }}</ref> The DRC Ministry of Public Health also requested that the US trial mAb114 treatment during the outbreak. The [[mAb114]] [[monoclonal antibody]] was developed by the [[National Institutes of Health]] and [[Jean-Jacques Muyembe-Tamfum|Jean-Jacques Muyembe]] at the National Institute for Biomedical Research, and is derived from an EVD survivor of the 1995 [[Kikwit]] outbreak who still had circulating anti-Ebola antibodies eleven years later; it has been tested in [[macaque]]s but not in humans.<ref name=ProMED_24_May_2018>{{cite web|title=ProMED-mail post|url=http://www.promedmail.org/direct.php?id=20180524.5816349|website=www.promedmail.org|access-date=25 May 2018|language=en}}</ref><ref>{{cite journal |author=Erika Check Hayden |title=Ebola survivor's blood holds promise of new treatment |journal=[[Nature (journal)|Nature]] |date=25 February 2016 |doi=10.1038/nature.2016.19440|s2cid=211835755 }}</ref><ref>{{cite journal |author=Davide Corti |display-authors=et al |title=Protective monotherapy against lethal Ebola virus infection by a potently neutralizing antibody |journal=[[Science (journal)|Science]] |year=2016 |volume=351 |issue=6279 |pages=1339–42 |doi=10.1126/science.aad5224 |pmid=26917593 |bibcode=2016Sci...351.1339C |doi-access=free }}</ref>

The [[ZMapp]] cocktail was assessed by the World Health Organization for emergency use under the [[Monitored Emergency Use of Unregistered and Investigational Interventions|MEURI]] ethical protocol. The panel agreed that "the benefits of ZMapp outweigh its risks" while noting that it presented logistical challenges, particularly that of requiring a [[cold chain]] for distribution and storage.<ref name=MEURI>{{cite web|publisher=World Health Organization |title=Notes for the record: Consultation on Monitored Emergency Use of Unregistered and Investigational Interventions for Ebola Virus Disease (EVD) |url=https://www.who.int/emergencies/ebola/MEURI-Ebola.pdf |date=17 May 2018 |access-date=25 May 2018}}</ref> Despite being at earlier stages of development, three other therapies, [[mAb114]], [[remdesivir]] and REGN3470-3471-3479, were also approved for emergency use under MEURI and the Ministry of Health in the DRC.<ref name=MEURI /><ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.nih.gov/news-events/news-releases/nih-begins-testing-ebola-treatment-early-stage-trial|title=NIH begins testing Ebola treatment in early-stage trial|date=2018-05-23|work=National Institutes of Health (NIH)|access-date=2018-05-25|language=en}}</ref>

==Prognosis==
{{further|Post-Ebola virus syndrome}}
[[Post-Ebola virus syndrome]] affects those who have survived EVD infection; the resulting signs and symptoms can include [[muscle]] pain, eye problems and [[neurological]] problems.<ref>{{cite journal |last1=Burki |first1=Talha Khan |title=Post-Ebola syndrome |journal=The Lancet Infectious Diseases |date=July 2016 |volume=16 |issue=7 |pages=780–781 |doi=10.1016/S1473-3099(15)00259-5 |pmid=27352759 |doi-access=free }}</ref>

==History==
{{further|List of Ebola outbreaks}}
The Democratic Republic of the Congo (formerly Zaire) has had several previous EVD outbreaks since 1976,<ref>{{cite journal |last1=Rosello |first1=Alicia |last2=Mossoko |first2=Mathias |last3=Flasche |first3=Stefan |last4=Van Hoek |first4=Albert Jan |last5=Mbala |first5=Placide |last6=Camacho |first6=Anton |last7=Funk |first7=Sebastian |last8=Kucharski |first8=Adam |last9=Ilunga |first9=Benoit Kebela |last10=Edmunds |first10=W John |last11=Piot |first11=Peter |last12=Baguelin |first12=Marc |last13=Muyembe Tamfum |first13=Jean-Jacques |title=Ebola virus disease in the Democratic Republic of the Congo, 1976-2014 |journal=eLife |date=3 November 2015 |volume=4 |doi=10.7554/eLife.09015 |pmid=26525597 |pmc=4629279 |doi-access=free }}</ref><ref name="cdc01" /> which are summarised in the Table below. All have been located in the west or north of the country.<ref name=DON_23_May_2018 /> Three previous outbreaks (in 1976, 1977 and 2014) occurring in [[Équateur (former province)|former province of Équateur]], of which the current [[Province of Équateur|Équateur province]] forms part.<ref name=who1/>

In 2014, the WHO considered that the DRC was lagging behind the rest of Africa in [[List of countries by total health expenditure per capita|health expenditures]], at the relative rate of [[Geary–Khamis dollar|Intl$]]32 per head.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.who.int/countries/cod/en/ |title=Democratic Republic of the Congo |publisher=[[World Health Organization]] |access-date=24 May 2018 }}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.who.int/gho/countries/cod.pdf?ua=1 |title=Democratic Republic of the Congo: WHO statistical profile |publisher=[[World Health Organization]] |date= January 2015 |access-date=24 May 2018 }}</ref>

For the [[2017 Democratic Republic of the Congo Ebola virus outbreak]], the DRC regulatory authorities approved the use of the experimental rVSV-ZEBOV vaccine, but logistical issues delayed its implementation until the outbreak was already under control.<ref name=Cohen_Science_ /><ref name=Nature_18_May_2018 /><ref>{{cite web |url=http://apps.who.int/iris/bitstream/handle/10665/255645/EbolaDRC-09062017.pdf?sequence=1 |title=Ebola virus disease: Democratic Republic of the Congo: External Situation Report 22 |publisher=WHO |date=8 June 2017 |access-date=21 May 2018 }}</ref>

Shortly before the first cases of the 2018 Ebola outbreak, the country experienced a widespread [[cholera]] epidemic (June 2017 – spring 2018), which was the most serious in the country since 1994.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.doctorswithoutborders.org/node/71721 |title=DRC: MSF Treats 17,000 People in One of the Largest National Cholera Outbreaks Ever |publisher=[[Médecins Sans Frontières]] |date=27 September 2017 |access-date=24 May 2018 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180525063031/https://www.doctorswithoutborders.org/node/71721 |archive-date=25 May 2018 |url-status=dead |df=dmy-all }}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.doctorswithoutborders.org/article/drc-fighting-country%E2%80%99s-worst-cholera-epidemic-decades |title=DRC: Fighting the Country's Worst Cholera Epidemic in Decades |publisher=[[Médecins Sans Frontières]] |date=25 January 2018 |access-date=24 May 2018 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180525062933/https://www.doctorswithoutborders.org/article/drc-fighting-country%E2%80%99s-worst-cholera-epidemic-decades |archive-date=25 May 2018 |url-status=dead |df=dmy-all }}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.who.int/csr/don/02-march-2018-cholera-drc/en/ |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180302190202/http://www.who.int/csr/don/02-march-2018-cholera-drc/en/ |url-status=dead |archive-date=2 March 2018 |title=Cholera – Kinshasa, Democratic Republic of the Congo |publisher=[[World Health Organization]] |date=25 January 2018 |access-date=24 May 2018 }}</ref>
