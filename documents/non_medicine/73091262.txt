The '''Environmental Science Center''' is a research center at [[Qatar University]]<ref>{{Cite web |title=Research Centers {{!}} Qatar University |url=http://www.qu.edu.qa/about/research-centers |access-date=2023-02-21 |website=www.qu.edu.qa}}</ref> and was established in 1980<ref name=":0">{{Cite web |title=Environmental Science Center {{!}} Qatar University |url=http://www.qu.edu.qa/research/esc |access-date=2023-02-20 |website=www.qu.edu.qa}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |date=2021-09-29 |title=Qatar University joins Future Food Systems CRC |url=https://www.futurefoodsystems.com.au/qatar-university-joins-future-food-systems-crc/ |access-date=2023-02-21 |website=Future Food Systems |language=en-AU}}</ref><ref name=":1">{{Cite web |date=2015-10-06 |title=Environmental Science Center |url=https://archive.unescwa.org/environmental-science-center |access-date=2023-02-21 |website=United Nations Economic and Social Commission for Western Asia |language=en}}</ref> to promote environmental studies across the state of Qatar with main focus on marine science, atmospheric and biological sciences.<ref>{{Cite web |last=Jeff OBBARD, PhD |title=Head, Climate Change Impacts, Centre for Climate Research, Singapore. Visiting Professor, School of Water, Energy & Environment, Cranfield University, UK. |url=https://sg.linkedin.com/in/jeff-obbard-phd |website=LinkedIn}}</ref> For the past 18 years,<ref>{{Cite web |date=2020-07-22 |title=THE HAWKSBILL TURTLE "LEUCISTIC" PHENOMENON IS REGISTERED FOR THE FIRST TIME IN QATAR AND THE ARABIAN GULF |url=http://www.qu.edu.qa/newsroom/esc/The-Hawksbill-turtle-%22Leucistic%22-phenomenon-is-registered-for-the-first-time-in-Qatar-and-the-Arabian-Gulf |access-date=2023-02-21 |website=www.qu.edu.qa}}</ref> ESC monitored and studied Hawksbill turtle nesting sites in Qatar.<ref>{{Cite web |date=2020-07-08 |title=ESC-QU MONITORS HAWKSBILL TURTLE CONSERVATION IN QATAR, Qatar University Newsroom |url=http://www.qu.edu.qa/newsroom/esc/ESC%E2%80%93QU-monitors-hawksbill-turtle-conservation-in-Qatar |access-date=2020-07-08 |website=www.qu.edu.qa}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |title=QU PROTECTS HAWKSBILL TURTLES THROUGH QATAR TURTLE PROJECT |url=http://www.qu.edu.qa/newsroom/esc/QU-protects-hawksbill-turtles-through-Qatar-Turtle-Project |access-date=2023-02-21 |website=www.qu.edu.qa}}</ref>

== History ==

* in 1980 it was named Scientific and Applied Research Center (SARC).<ref name=":1" />
* in 2005 it was restructured and renamed Environmental Studies Center (ESC).
* in 2015, the business name was changed to Environmental Science Center (ESC) to better reflect the research-driven objectives.<ref name=":0" />

== Research clusters ==
The ESC has 3 major research clusters<ref>{{Cite web |title=Qatar University Institutional Repository |url=https://qspace.qu.edu.qa/handle/10576/66 |access-date=2023-02-23 |website=qspace.qu.edu.qa}}</ref> that cover areas of strategic importance to Qatar. The clusters are:

* Atmospheric sciences cluster
* Earth sciences cluster
* Marine sciences cluster with 2 majors:<ref>{{Cite web |title=Research {{!}} Qatar University |url=http://www.qu.edu.qa/research/esc/research |access-date=2023-02-20 |website=www.qu.edu.qa}}</ref>
** Terrestrial Ecology
** Physical and Chemical Oceanography

== UNESCO Chair in marine sciences ==
The first of its kind in the Arabian Gulf region, United Nations Educational, Scientific and Cultural Organization (UNESCO) have announced the establishment of the UNESCO Chair in marine sciences at QU's Environmental Science Center.<ref>{{Cite web |date=21 April 2022 |title=Qatar establishes a UNESCO Chair in Marine Sciences |url=https://www.unesco.org/en/articles/qatar-establishes-unesco-chair-marine-sciences |website=UNESCO}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |last= |first= |last2= |date=2020-12-08 |title=QU's Environmental Science Center to get UNESCO Chair in marine sciences |url=https://www.qatar-tribune.com/article/203494/NATION/QUs-Environmental-Science-Center-to-get-UNESCO-Chair-in-marine-sciences |access-date=2023-02-21 |website=Qatar Tribune |language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |last=QNA/Doha |date=2020-12-08 |title=Unesco Chair in marine sciences set up at QU |url=https://www.gulf-times.com/story/679802/unesco-chair-in-marine-sciences-set-up-at-qu |access-date=2023-02-21 |website=Gulf Times |language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |last=Qatar University Research Magazine, Issue 17, P. 24 |date=May 2022 |title=UNESCO Chair in Marine Sciences at Qatar University |url=https://www.qu.edu.qa/static_file/qu/research/magazine/indexing/issue-17/english/24.pdf |website=Magazine issues, Qatar University}}</ref> The chair is aiming to providing sustainable marine environment in the Arabian Gulf and protection of marine ecosystems.<ref>{{Cite web |last= |first= |date=2020-12-08 |title=Unesco Chair in Marine Sciences established at Qatar University |url=https://thepeninsulaqatar.com/article/08/12/2020/Unesco-Chair-in-Marine-Sciences-established-at-Qatar-University |access-date=2023-02-21 |website=thepeninsulaqatar.com |language=en}}</ref>

== Accreditation ==
The ESC labs have been granted [[ISO/IEC 17025]] by American Association of Laboratory Accreditation (A2LA), affirming their status as world-class facilities operating to best practice.<ref>{{Cite web |title=QU RESEARCH LABS ACHIEVE RENEWAL OF ISO/IEC - 17025:2005 ACCREDITATION |url=http://www.qu.edu.qa/newsroom/CBE/QU-research-labs-achieve-renewal-of-ISO-IEC-%E2%80%93-17025:2005-accreditation |access-date=2023-02-21 |website=www.qu.edu.qa}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |title=Accreditation {{!}} Qatar University |url=http://www.qu.edu.qa/research/esc/about/accreditation |access-date=2023-02-20 |website=www.qu.edu.qa}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |title=RESEARCH LABS ACHIEVE ISO 17025 ACCREDITATION |url=http://www.qu.edu.qa/newsroom/Qatar-University/Research-labs-achieve-ISO-17025-accreditation |access-date=2023-02-21 |website=www.qu.edu.qa}}</ref>

== Facilities ==
[[File:ESC-010704.jpg|alt=Janan Research Vessel - Qatar University|thumb|JANAN Research Vessel used by ESC since 2011 - Qatar University<ref name=":2" />]]ESC is the home of wide range of facilities. The most notable one is the mobile labs on board the JANAN Research Vessel.<ref>{{Cite web |last=JANAN RV, IMO 9488906 |title=BalticShipping.com |url=https://www.balticshipping.com/vessel/imo/9488906 |access-date=2023-02-21 |website=www.balticshipping.com |language=en}}</ref><ref name=":3">{{Cite web |title=JANAN Research Vessel {{!}} Qatar University |url=http://www.qu.edu.qa/research/esc/facilities/janan |access-date=2023-02-20 |website=www.qu.edu.qa}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |title=Ship JANAN RV (Research/Survey Vessel) Registered in Qatar - Vessel details, Current position and Voyage information - IMO 9488906, MMSI 466078000, Call Sign A7D6649 |url=https://www.marinetraffic.com/en/ais/details/ships/shipid:678881/mmsi:466078000/imo:9488906/vessel:JANAN_RV |access-date=2023-02-21 |website=MarineTraffic.com |language=en}}</ref>

JANAN is a 42.80 m. multipurpose Research Vessel<ref name=":2">{{Cite web |title=Janan RV |url=https://freireshipyard.com/en/buque/oceanographic-offshore/janan-rv/ |access-date=2023-02-21 |website=Freire Shipyard |language=en-US}}</ref> and was named after the island located in the western coast of the Qatari peninsula. It was donated to Qatar University by H.H.  [[Tamim bin Hamad Al Thani|Sheikh Tamim bin Hamad Al Thani]]<ref name=":3" /> the Amir of Qatar.

JANAN is used extensively in studying the state of marine environment in the Exclusive Economic Zone (EEZ) of the State of Qatar<ref>{{Cite web |last= |first= |date=2021-04-13 |title=Janan vessel begins research journey |url=https://thepeninsulaqatar.com/article/13/04/2021/Janan-vessel-begins-research-journey |access-date=2023-02-21 |website=thepeninsulaqatar.com |language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |date=2021-04-11 |title=Research vessel begins three-day trip |url=https://www.gulf-times.com/story/688723/research-vessel-begins-three-day-trip |access-date=2023-02-21 |website=Gulf Times |language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |title=Janan Research Vessel Begins Trip to Study Marine Environment in Regional Waters |url=http://archive.qatarday.com/news/nature/janan-research-vessel-begins-trip-to-study-marine-environment-in-regional-waters/87594 |access-date=2023-02-21 |website=Qatar Day |language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |last=Network |first=W. G. O. |date=2020-02-19 |title=Research vessel Janan departs to study the marine environment |url=https://wgoqatar.com/2020/02/research-vessel-janan-departs-to-study-the-marine-environment/ |access-date=2023-02-21 |website=What's Goin On Qatar |language=en-US}}</ref> and to advance critical marine environmental studies and research in Qatar and the wider Gulf.

The center also has 12 labs equipped with state-of-arts instruments.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Labs {{!}} Qatar University |url=http://www.qu.edu.qa/research/esc/facilities/labs |access-date=2023-02-20 |website=www.qu.edu.qa}}</ref>
