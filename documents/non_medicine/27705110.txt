'''Third Productions''' is an independent production house with an emphasis on creating progressive [[Media (communication)|media]]. Third creates [[music video]]s, [[web series]], and hosts live events.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://blogs.phoenixnewtimes.com/uponsun/2012/05/captain_squeegee_mcc.php |title=Captain Squeegee Teams with MCC's Big Band Tonight - Phoenix Music - Up on the Sun |website=blogs.phoenixnewtimes.com |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120514171827/http://blogs.phoenixnewtimes.com/uponsun/2012/05/captain_squeegee_mcc.php |archive-date=2012-05-14}}</ref><nowiki/>

==Web series==

===Lucidity===
{{See also|Lucidity (web series)}}
Lucidity: The Web Saga is a metaphysical comedy web series produced by Third Productions. The program was created and written by Sean Oliver and Danny Torgersen, who co-star as George (Torgersen) and Jason (Oliver).<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.imdb.com/title/tt2247625/|title = Lucidity Web Saga|website = [[IMDb]]|date = 4 January 2010}}</ref> The series begins with two roommates, George and Jason, who for a reasons unknown to them share each other's dreams. The two have no control over their dreams and as the astral world increasingly encroaches on their real lives they decide to take action. This decision sends the roommates on an out-of-body adventure that neither were prepared for.<ref>{{Cite book |last=Terrace |first=Vincent |url=https://books.google.com/books?id=71GuAwAAQBAJ&dq=lucidity+the+web+saga&pg=PA134 |title=Internet Horror, Science Fiction and Fantasy Television Series, 1998-2013 |date=2014-05-14 |publisher=McFarland |isbn=978-1-4766-1645-2 |language=en}}</ref>

Lucidity was screened at the LAWeb Fest 2011, 2012, and 2013. Winning awards in Editing, Directing, Music Composition, and Special Effects.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.lawebfest.com/ |title=Home |website=lawebfest.com |access-date=2022-08-08 |archive-date=2015-02-07 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150207003314/http://lawebfest.com/ |url-status=dead }}</ref> The saga was also an official selection at Phoenix Comicon 2013.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://blogs.phoenixnewtimes.com/uponsun/2013/05/captain-squeegee-lucidity-comicon-interview.php |title=Captain Squeegee: "Music Is Just Gonna Be Free! I'm Okay with That!" |website=blogs.phoenixnewtimes.com |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20131029094311/http://blogs.phoenixnewtimes.com/uponsun/2013/05/captain-squeegee-lucidity-comicon-interview.php |archive-date=2013-10-29}}</ref>

===Diamond Hunter===
Diamond Hunter is a 7 episode docu-series about three friends (Nick Montefour, Peter Mikelis, and Hendrik Chasse) risking everything they have trying to strike it rich mining diamonds in Africa.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.imdb.com/title/tt2698194/|title = Diamond Hunter|website = [[IMDb]]|date = 31 October 2012}}</ref>

Diamond Hunter was an Official Selection at LAWeb Fest 2013.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.lawebfest.com/ |title=Home |website=lawebfest.com |access-date=2022-08-08 |archive-date=2015-02-07 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150207003314/http://lawebfest.com/ |url-status=dead }}</ref> Winning multiple awards: Outstanding Series, Editing, and Writing.

===But Vessels===
But Vessels is self described as, "a semi-satirical trans-temporal, cross dimensional, vibratory complex that expresses itself as a Rock Band." Forming in 2012 the comedy band has released several music videos and is currently recording their first EP.<ref>{{cite web | url=http://www.echocloud.tv/pretentious-hippies-top-5-anticipated-arizona-albums-of-2013/ | title=Echocloud.tv }}</ref>
