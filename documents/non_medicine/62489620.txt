==During the games==
===Scheduling===
* Philippines athletics head coach Sean Guevara was furious with the scheduling of [[Athletics at the 2017 Southeast Asian Games|athletics programme]] as their athletes would compete in the same event on the same day in the space of 30 minutes in the heats and just one hour apart in 100m and 400m finals causing the [[Philippine Athletics Track and Field Association]] (PATAFA) to request for a change or adjustment to the schedule for the men's 400m hurdles and 100m, but the Malaysian organising committee did not take any action when the [[Olympic Council of Malaysia]] (OCM) secretary-general Low Beng Choo explained he had noticed the [[Philippine Olympic Committee]] (POC) appeal for the schedule to be changed but the Malaysian Athletics Technical Delegate turned the request down.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.thestar.com.my/sport/athletics/2017/08/10/trouble-at-the-track-the-philippines-cry-foul-over-athletics-programme-schedule/|title=The Philippines cry foul over athletics programme schedule|author=Aftar Singh|work=The Star|date=10 August 2017|access-date=18 August 2017|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170818150104/http://www.thestar.com.my/sport/athletics/2017/08/10/trouble-at-the-track-the-philippines-cry-foul-over-athletics-programme-schedule/|archive-date=18 August 2017|url-status=dead}}</ref>
* The [[Singapore national under-22 football team]] coach [[Richard Tardy]] criticised the organisers for informing him about a press conference held on 13 August just 30 minutes prior to its scheduled 11:30am start time, causing him to be late. [[Thailand national under-22 football team]] coach [[Worrawoot Srimaka]] also failed to turn up as he was informed about the conference only after having to reschedule his team's training session.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://sg.news.yahoo.com/singapore-coach-slams-sea-games-organisers-scheduling-issues-092540570.html|title=Singapore coach slams SEA Games organisers over scheduling issues|author=Nigel Chin|publisher=Yahoo! I am asking this on behalf of all other ASEAN countries, not just for [[Myanmar national under-22 football team|Myanmar]]”.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.mmtimes.com/index.php/sports/27286-myanmar-fans-upset-by-malaysia-s-failure-to-air-some-football-matches.html|title=Myanmar fans upset by Malaysia's failure to air some football matches|author=Kyaw Zin Hlaing|publisher=The Myanmar Times|date=16 August 2017|access-date=18 August 2017|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170818114657/http://www.mmtimes.com/index.php/sports/27286-myanmar-fans-upset-by-malaysia-s-failure-to-air-some-football-matches.html|archive-date=18 August 2017|url-status=dead}}</ref>

===Judging issues===
* The failure of Malaysian SEA Games Organising Committee (MASOC) to fully install “video challenge” or “dark fish” system for [[Sepak takraw at the 2017 Southeast Asian Games|sepak takraw matches]] that can be used by teams to challenge calls made by the umpire or [[referee]] during a bout or match are criticised by the national sepak takraw association itself as all ASEAN sepak takraw teams had been notified and expecting the technology to be used in the competition.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.themalaymailonline.com/sports/article/psm-disappointed-with-sea-games-committee-for-failure-to-install-video-chal|title=PSM disappointed with SEA Games committee for failure to install 'video challenge'|work=Bernama|publisher=The Malay Mail|date=18 August 2017|access-date=18 August 2017}}</ref>
* On 20 August, Indonesian women's sepak takraw team walked out of the venue to protest a call by the referee. Following the match abandonment from the Indonesian side, the Malaysian side was awarded a 2–0 score by the referee.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.channelnewsasia.com/news/sport/sea-games-sepak-takraw-competition-marred-by-indonesian-walkout-9141804|title=SEA Games: Sepak takraw competition marred by Indonesian walkout|work=Bernama|publisher=Channel NewsAsia|date=21 August 2017|access-date=21 August 2017}}</ref> Later, Malaysian media claimed that Indonesia has admitted that it was a mistake to abandon the match.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.themalaymailonline.com/sports/article/indonesias-sepak-takraw-association-admits-mistake-after-yesterdays-walkout|title=Indonesia's sepak takraw association admits mistake after yesterday's walkout|work=Bernama|publisher=The Malay Mail|date=21 August 2017|access-date=21 August 2017}}</ref> However, this statement was rejected by the Indonesian officials.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.viva.co.id/sport/gelanggang/948895-tim-sepak-takraw-indonesia-bantah-akui-kesalahan|title=Tim Sepak Takraw Indonesia Bantah Akui Kesalahan|trans-title=Indonesia sepak takraw team denies they have admitted mistake over walk out|author=Riki Ilham Rafles|language=id|publisher=Viva.co.id|access-date=22 August 2017|date=22 August 2017}}</ref>
* During [[Pencak silat at the 2017 Southeast Asian Games|pencak silat men's doubles seni contest]] on 24 August, Malaysian pair Taqiyuddin Hamid and Rosli Sharif won the competition with 582 points. Indonesian coaches, whose athlete Hendy and Yolla Primadona settled for silver, claimed that the given score is not natural and biased. According to one of the coaches, the highest point ever given for the competition was around 570.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://sport.tempo.co/read/news/2017/08/24/103902806/kontroversi-sea-games-2017-pesilat-indonesia-dicurangi-wasit|title=Kontroversi SEA Games 2017: Pesilat Indonesia Dicurangi Wasit|trans-title=2017 SEA Games Controversy: Indonesian silat exponent cheated by referee|language=id|work=[[Antara (news agency)|Antara]]|publisher=[[Tempo (Indonesian magazine)|Tempo]]|date=24 August 2017|access-date=25 August 2017}}</ref> However, Malaysian National Silat Federation secretary general Datuk Megat Zulkarnain Omardin has denied such claims and said that the Indonesian team had accepted the result during team manager meeting.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.nst.com.my/sports/others/2017/08/272735/kl2017-pesaka-refutes-claims-biased-silat-judging|title=KL2017: Pesaka refutes claims of biased silat judging|author=Ferzalfie Fauzi|publisher=New Straits Times|date=25 August 2017|access-date=25 August 2017}}</ref> Indonesian Minister of Youth and Sport Affairs, Imam Nahrawi, later said that he was planning on sending protest notes to the Asian Pencak Silat Federation and the Olympic Council of Asia (OCA) to prevent the referees or judges from officiating in [[2018 Asian Games]] to be held in Indonesia.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://en.tempo.co/read/news/2017/08/25/068903244/SEA-Games:-Govt-to-Send-Protest-Notes-over-Controversial-Referees|title=SEA Games: Govt to Send Protest Notes over Controversial Referees|author=Didit Hariyadi|publisher=Tempo|date=25 August 2017|access-date=25 August 2017}}</ref>
* During [[Pencak silat at the 2017 Southeast Asian Games|Pencak Silat - Tanding Men Class D]] on 29 August, Malaysian athlete Razak Ghani seriously injured in the [[rib]] during a match against Thai athlete Pornteb Poolkaew causing it to be stopped definitely. The referee however gave Razak to win the match in a sudden controversial decision.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.thairath.co.th/content/1054085|title=ฉาวส่งท้าย! ปันจักสีลัตไทยหน้าจืด เจ้าภาพเจ็บแต่ได้ทอง อ้างเหตุสุดแสบ (คลิป)|trans-title=Scandalous! Thai Pencak silat sad, host was injured but get a gold medal in a bad decision (Video)|language=th|publisher=Thairath.co.th|date=29 August 2017|access-date=29 August 2017}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://sport.trueid.net/detail/100074|title=โดนปรับแพ้! ปันจักสีลัตไทย ซัดเจ้าภาพ บาดเจ็บแข่งต่อไม่ได้ แต่ชวดเหรียญทอง (มีคลิป)|trans-title=Beaten up! Thai Pencak silat success in beating Malaysian, but unsuccessful to get a gold medal (Video)|language=th|publisher=trueID|date=29 August 2017|access-date=29 August 2017}}</ref>
* The results of the [[Boxing at the 2017 Southeast Asian Games – Results#Men's light flyweight|boxing men's light flyweight]] quarter-final bout between Carlo Paalam of Philippines and Muhamad Fuad Redzuan of the host nation Malaysia was met with criticism as the latter was given a 5–0 decision despite being out boxed. Muhamad Fuad was also warned by the referee for illegal attacks such as take downs, headlocks and occasional lacing of his opponent's face in clinches but received only warnings and no point deductions. [[Association of Boxing Alliances in the Philippines]] (ABAP) executive director Ed Picson declined to make comment about the officiating and scoring other than to say "I thought it was the other way around. But it is what it is".<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.rappler.com/sports/specials/sea-games/179475-carlo-paalam-muhamad-redzuan-boxing-dubious|title=PH boxer Paalam ousted by Malaysian in dubious decision at 2017 SEA Games|author=Ryan Songalia|publisher=Rappler|date=21 August 2017|access-date=21 August 2017}}</ref> However, Muhamad Fuad gets to prove his worth when he went on to win the semi-final and final bouts to take the event gold medal.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.nst.com.my/sports/others/2017/08/272145/kl2017-muhamad-fuad-deliver-malaysias-first-sea-games-gold-boxing-2009|title=KL2017: Muhamad Fuad deliver Malaysia's first Sea Games gold in boxing since 2009|author=Fardy Bungga|publisher=New Straits Times|date=24 August 2017|access-date=26 August 2017}}</ref>
* The winner of the women's 10,000 metres walk race, Elena Goh Ling Yin from the host nation Malaysia, was accused of cheating. The Vietnamese media protested that the Malaysian athlete cheated by "running" instead of "walking", especially during the last lap to overtake Phan Thị Bích Hà from Vietnam. According to walk races' rules, athletes’ two feet are not allowed to leave the ground at the same time.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.thoughtco.com/olympic-race-walking-rules-3258723|title=See: Learn the Basic Rules for Olympic Race Walking|publisher=ThoughtCo|access-date=26 August 2017}}</ref> The rules also state that the front leg must straighten when it makes contact with the ground. A violation may be cautioned with a yellow paddle, while repeat violations may be met with a red card. Three red cards, from three different judges, will result in a competitor's disqualification. Pictures allegedly showed Elena Goh violating both of these rules.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://chirpstory.com/li/367123|title=Karena Foto Ini Atlet Malaysia, Elena Goh Diduga Curang Di Cabor Jalan Cepat Putri SEA Games 2017|trans-title=Because of this photo, Malaysian athlete Elena Goh is suspected of cheating in 2017 SEA Games Women's race walking event|language=id|publisher=Chirpstory|access-date=26 August 2017}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://dtinews.vn/en/news/023002/52483/malaysian-accused-of-cheating-in-sea-games-walk-gold.html|title=Malaysian accused of cheating in SEA Games walk gold|publisher=Dân Trí|date=25 August 2017|access-date=26 August 2017}}</ref> Meanwhile, the runner-up Phan Thị Bích Hà said to the media that, "It's too frustrating. But I couldn't do anything. As a competitor, I won't make any comment. But everyone can clearly see what happened on the track".<ref>{{cite web|url=http://thethao.vnexpress.net/tin-tuc/cac-mon-khac/viet-nam-mat-hc-vang-vi-vdv-chu-nha-chay-o-mon-di-bo-3631750.html|title=Việt Nam mất HC vàng vì VĐV chủ nhà chạy ở môn đi bộ|trans-title=Vietnam loses gold because one athlete was running|author=Lâm Thỏa|language=vi|publisher=[[VnExpress]]|date=23 August 2017|access-date=23 August 2017}}</ref>

===Ticketing issues===
* The allocated tickets of the Myanmar-[[Laos national under-22 football team|Laos]] football men's group match at the [[UiTM Stadium]] which was said to be already sold out causing Myanmar fans had to support their team from outside the stadium despite there are still many empty spaces available inside the stadium as they were not allowed to enter.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.malaysiakini.com/columns/392590|title=Did we screw up the SEA Games, Khairy|author=Fa Abdul|publisher=[[Malaysiakini]]|date=22 August 2017|access-date=23 August 2017}}</ref>
* On 28 August, many Malaysians fans angered over ticket sales system at the [[Shah Alam Stadium]] for the [[Football at the 2017 Southeast Asian Games – Men's tournament|final football match between Malaysia and Thailand]]. Ticket counters at the Shah Alam Stadium are only open up to 2 counters resulting in chaos and forced [[Federal Reserve Unit]] (FRU) and police to be deployed to the area to disperse the crowd involved in the chaos. Most of those involved are frustrated because they have long queues since 6am but unable to buy even one ticket as the counter had run out of ticket sales. Other Malaysian fans also criticises the ticketing system as it is not sold online.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.nst.com.my/sports/football/2017/08/273835/fans-turned-violent-sea-games-football-final-tickets-sold-out|title=Fans turned violent as Sea Games football final tickets sold out|author1=Beatrice Nita Jay|author2=Faliq Lajim|author3=Izzudin Aznan|publisher=New Straits Times|date=28 August 2017|access-date=29 August 2017}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.channelnewsasia.com/news/sport/sea-games-tempers-flare-at-shah-alam-stadium-as-thousands-miss-9164944|title=SEA Games: Tempers flare at Shah Alam Stadium as thousands miss out on football final tickets|publisher=Channel NewsAsia|date=28 August 2017|access-date=29 August 2017}}</ref> But Malaysian Minister of Youth and Sports, Khairy Jamaluddin explained that online payment gateway provider could not guarantee a stable process because of volume and they were concerned people would transact payment and not get tickets because of the sheer volume.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.themalaymailonline.com/sports/article/on-twitter-khairy-explains-why-tickets-for-sea-games-football-final-not-sol|title=On Twitter, Khairy explains why tickets for SEA Games football final not sold online|publisher=The Malay Mail|date=28 August 2017|access-date=29 August 2017}}</ref>

==After the games==
===Doping===
* It was reported on 26 October 2017 that Malaysian diver Wendy Ng Yan Yee had failed a doping test conducted at the Games. Her B sample tested positive for [[sibutramine]], a stimulant banned by the [[World Anti-Doping Agency]] (WADA). As a consequence, Malaysia will lose the gold medal won by Wendy Ng and fellow diver Dhabitah in the 3m springboard synchronised event.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.straitstimes.com/sport/sea-games-spore-in-line-for-surprise-diving-gold-after-malaysia-diver-who-failed-doping-test|title=SEA Games: Singapore in line for surprise diving gold after Malaysian diver who failed doping test is identified|author=Jonathan Wong|publisher=The Straits Times|date=26 October 2017|access-date=27 October 2017}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.thestar.com.my/sport/diving/2017/10/26/diver-wendy-fails-doping-test/|title=Diver Wendy Ng fails doping test|author=Lim Teik Huat|work=The Star|date=26 October 2017|access-date=27 October 2017}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.freemalaysiatoday.com/category/nation/2017/10/26/diver-ng-loses-sea-games-gold-medals-after-failing-doping-test/|title=Diver Ng loses SEA Games gold medals after failing doping test|publisher=Free Malaysia Today|date=26 October 2017|access-date=27 October 2017}}</ref>
