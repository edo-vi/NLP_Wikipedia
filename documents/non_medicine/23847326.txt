'''VCA Animal Hospitals''', incorporated as '''VCA, Inc.''', operates more than 1,000 animal hospitals in the US and Canada.<ref name="Wogan 2020">{{cite journal |last1=Wogan |first1=Lisa |title=Washington state veterinary practice ratifies union contract |url=https://news.vin.com/default.aspx?pid=210&Id=9789104 |website=Vin.com |access-date=18 September 2020 |date=August 27, 2020}}</ref> The company is based in [[Los Angeles]], and was founded in 1986.<ref>{{Cite journal|url=http://news.vin.com/vinnews.aspx?articleId=32842|title=VCA Inc.: Gentle giant or takeover threat?|last=Lau|first=Edie|date=2014-07-11|website=Vin.com|access-date=2018-11-08}}</ref> Until its acquisition by [[Mars, Incorporated|Mars]] in 2017, VCA traded on the [[NASDAQ]] under the ticker "WOOF".<ref name="Clenfield">{{Cite news|url=https://www.bloomberg.com/news/features/2017-01-05/when-big-business-happens-to-your-pet|title=The High-Cost, High-Risk World of Modern Pet Care|last=Clenfield|first=Jason|date=2017-01-05|work=Bloomberg|access-date=2018-11-08}}</ref>

VCA was founded in 1986 by three health care company executives,<ref name="Larkin 2017">{{Cite news|url=https://www.avma.org/News/JAVMANews/Pages/170301a.aspx|title=Veterinarians Incorporated|last=Larkin|first=Malinda|date=2017-03-01|work=JAVMA News|access-date=2018-11-08}}</ref> Neil Tauber and brothers Robert (Bob) and Arthur (Art) Antin.<ref name="Clenfield"/><ref name="Peltz 2017" /> The name is an abbreviation of "Veterinary Centers of America." VCA acquired its first veterinary clinic, West Los Angeles Veterinary Hospital, in 1987.<ref name="Larkin 2017" />

In October 2004, VCA purchased Sound Technologies,<ref name="Lau 2009">{{Cite news|url=https://news.vin.com/VINNews.aspx?articleId=13338|title=VCA Antech expands its foray into medical technology by buying Eklin|last=Lau|first=Edie|date=2009-07-11|work=VIN News Service|access-date=2018-11-08}}</ref> a company which supplied digital radiology and ultrasound equipment to veterinary hospitals.<ref>{{Cite news |title=VCA Antech, Inc. Announces Acquisition of Sound Technologies, Inc. |url=https://www.businesswire.com/news/home/20041004005436/en/VCA-Antech-Announces-Acquisition-Sound-Technologies |access-date=2018-11-08 |work=BusinessWire |date=October 4, 2004 }}</ref> In July 2009, VCA purchased diagnostic imaging company Eklin Medical Systems,<ref name="Lau 2009" /> which was combined with Sound Technologies to form Sound/Antech Imaging Services.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://news.vin.com/vinnews.aspx?articleId=45149|title=The dark side of modern X-ray equipment|last=Lau|first=Edie|date=2017-06-08|work=VIN News Service|access-date=2018-11-08}}</ref>

In 2014, VCA acquired the dog day care center Camp Bow Wow, which had about 140 franchises in the US and one in Canada<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.denverpost.com/2017/06/12/camp-bow-wows-westminster-office/|title=Camp Bow Wow's new corporate offices in Westminster take dog-friendly to the next level|last=Rusch|first=Emilie|date=2017-06-12|work=[[The Denver Post]]|access-date=2018-11-08}}</ref> and licensed The Barkley Pet Hotel & Day Spa brand in Westlake Village, California from Beverly Hills Pet Hotels, Inc. 

On January 9, 2017, [[Mars Inc.]] announced its intention to purchase VCA for $9.1 billion, operating it as an independent unit within [[Mars Petcare]].<ref name="Peltz 2017">{{Cite news|url=https://www.latimes.com/business/la-fi-vca-mars-20170109-story.html|title=Candy maker Mars is buying L.A. animal hospital chain VCA for $7.7 billion|last=Peltz|first=James F.|date=9 January 2017|work=[[Los Angeles Times]]|access-date=10 August 2017}}</ref> VCA CEO Bob Antin remained in his position.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.businessinsider.com/mars-to-buy-vca-2017-1|title=Mars is buying a pet health company for $9.1 billion|last=Crowe|first=Portia|date=9 January 2017|work=[[Business Insider]]|access-date=10 August 2017}}</ref>
