'''Anwar El Ghazi''' ({{lang-ar|ÿ£ŸÜŸàÿ± ÿßŸÑÿ∫ÿßÿ≤Ÿä}}; born 3 May 1995) is a Dutch professional [[association football|footballer]] who most recently played for [[Bundesliga]] club [[1. FSV Mainz 05|Mainz 05]]. He plays as a [[Forward (association football)#Winger|winger]] or a [[Forward (association football)#Inside forward|forward]].

He had previously played for [[AFC Ajax|Ajax]], [[Lille OSC|Lille]] and [[Aston Villa FC|Aston Villa]], and [[PSV Eindhoven|PSV]].

==Club career==
===Early career===
El Ghazi began his football career in the youth ranks of his local club [[BVV Barendrecht]], joining the youth academy of [[Feyenoord]] for two seasons. He then joined the ranks of Spartaan '20 before being recruited to [[Sparta Rotterdam]], where he progressed through the academy. In 2013, he joined the [[Ajax Youth Academy]], playing for the A1 selection (under-19), competing in the [[UEFA Youth League]].<ref>[http://www.rijnmond.nl/sport/06-05-2014/voormalig-sparta-talent-el-ghazi-mikt-op-ajax-1 Voormalig Sparta-talent El-Ghazi mikt op Ajax 1], Rijnmond.nl, 6 May 2014</ref>

===Ajax===
[[File:Dnepr-Ajax (1).jpg|thumb|upright|El Ghazi playing for [[AFC Ajax|Ajax]] in 2015]]

El Ghazi was signed by [[AFC Ajax|Ajax]] in July 2013.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ajax.nl/Ajax-Nieuws/Ajax-Nieuws/Ajax-nieuws-artikel/190832/Ajax-contracteert-Anwar-ElGhazi.htm|title=Ajax contracteert Anwar El-Ghazi|date=16 April 2013|publisher=AFC Ajax|access-date=3 August 2014}}</ref> During the [[2014‚Äì15 AFC Ajax season|2014‚Äì15 pre-season]], he scored 8 goals in 150 minutes of playing time, finishing as Ajax's top pre-season scorer. While Ajax initially intended to send El Ghazi to their reserve team, Jong Ajax, El Ghazi's pre-season prompted Ajax to include him in the first-team squad, following the loan of fellow winger [[Lesley de Sa]] to [[Go Ahead Eagles]]. El Ghazi made his official debut for Ajax in the [[2014 Johan Cruyff Shield]] against [[PEC Zwolle]], coming on for [[Ricardo Kishna]] in the 1‚Äì0 loss at home.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://int.soccerway.com/matches/2014/08/03/netherlands/super-cup/bvo-fc-zwolle/afc-ajax/1676573/|title=PEC Zwolle vs. Ajax|date=3 August 2014|publisher=Soccerway|access-date=3 August 2014}}</ref>

El Ghazi made his regular season debut for Ajax in the [[2014‚Äì15 Eredivisie]] season opener against [[SBV Vitesse|Vitesse]]. The match ended in a 4‚Äì1 home victory, with El Ghazi providing the assist for the fourth goal by [[Lasse Sch√∂ne]] in the 87th minute.<ref>[https://int.soccerway.com/matches/2014/08/10/netherlands/eredivisie/afc-ajax/sbv-vitesse/1690489/ Ajax vs. Vitesse 4‚Äì1], Soccerway.co, 10 August 2014</ref> On 17 August 2014, El Ghazi scored his first [[Eredivisie]] goal in the 3‚Äì1 away win against [[AZ Alkmaar|AZ]], scoring in the 90th minute.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://int.soccerway.com/matches/2014/08/17/netherlands/eredivisie/stichting-az/afc-ajax/1690503/|title=AZ vs. Ajax|date=17 August 2014|publisher=Soccerway|access-date=17 August 2014}}</ref> He scored his first [[UEFA Champions League]] goal on 22 October 2014 at [[Camp Nou]] against [[FC Barcelona|Barcelona]] in the 88th minute of a 3‚Äì1 loss. In so doing, he became the first player to score at Camp Nou since the beginning of the [[2014‚Äì15 FC Barcelona season|2014‚Äì15 season]], when Barcelona went five matches at home without conceding a goal.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.soccernews.nl/news/281679/El_Ghazi_zorgt_voor_opmerkelijke_statistiek|title=El Ghazi zorgt voor opmerkelijke statistiek|date=22 October 2014|publisher=Soccernews.nl|access-date=22 October 2014}}</ref>

===Aston Villa===
On 22 August 2018, El Ghazi signed for [[EFL Championship|Championship]] club [[Aston Villa F.C.|Aston Villa]] on a season-long loan, with a clause to buy included.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.losc.fr/actualites-foot-lille/el-ghazi-pr%C3%AAt%C3%A9-%C3%A0-aston-villa|title=El Ghazi pr√™t√© √† Aston Villa {{!}} Lille LOSC|website=www.losc.fr|language=fr|access-date=22 August 2018}}</ref> He made his debut on 25 August 2018 in a 1‚Äì1 draw with [[Reading F.C.|Reading]], providing an assist by crossing the ball to [[Ahmed Elmohamady]], who scored a header.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/45229935|title=Aston Villa 1‚Äì1 Reading|date=25 August 2018|work=[[BBC Sport]]|access-date=25 August 2018}}</ref> He scored his first goal for the Villains a week later on 1 September 2018 in a 4‚Äì1 defeat to [[Sheffield United]].

This occurred after El Ghazi allegedly struck Leeds forward [[Patrick Bamford]] during an on pitch brawl following [[Mateusz Klich]]'s controversial 72nd opening goal for Leeds United following [[Tyler Roberts]]' refusal to put the ball out of touch for an injury.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/48000922|title=Leeds United 1‚Äì1 Aston Villa |date=28 April 2019|work=[[BBC Sport]]|access-date=29 April 2019}}</ref> The decision was met with controversy from fans and pundits alike, with Aston Villa head coach [[Dean Smith (footballer, born 1971)|Dean Smith]] claiming that after reviewing the footage of the incident reported that he'd be 'amazed' if the red card and subsequent three-game ban weren't overturned after appeal.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.skysports.com/football/news/11677/11706832/aston-villa-to-appeal-anwar-el-ghazi-red-card-shown-during-leeds-draw|title=Aston Villa to appeal Anwar El Ghazi red card shown during Leeds draw|date=28 April 2019|work=[[Sky Sports]]|access-date=29 April 2019}}</ref> The red card and ban were later rescinded and on 2 May, Bamford was banned for two games for "successful deception of a match official".<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.theguardian.com/football/2019/apr/30/leeds-patrick-bamford-charged-aston-villa-anwar-el-ghazi|title=Leeds striker Patrick Bamford banned for two games for deceiving referee|last=Taylor|first=Louise|date=2 May 2019|work=The Guardian|access-date=3 May 2019}}</ref>

==International career==
Having dual citizenship, El Ghazi was eligible to represent either the [[Netherlands national football team|Netherlands]] or [[Morocco national football team|Morocco]] at senior level. He made his international debut playing for the [[Netherlands national under-18 football team|Netherlands under-18]] side in a friendly match against [[Austria national under-18 football team|Austria]] on 15 October 2012, a 2‚Äì0 loss.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.onsoranje.nl/team-statistieken/teams/speler/276/50a3c21a-21d9e940-289960|title=Speler: Anwar El-Ghazi|publisher=OnsOranje|access-date=5 August 2014}}</ref>

===Netherlands===
El Ghazi met [[Cristiano Ronaldo]] during the summer break, a player who he looks up to, and asked him whether he should represent the Netherlands or Morocco. Ronaldo advised him to choose the Netherlands because he would have a higher chance to participate in an international tournament, based on their history. Consequently, El Ghazi said he would choose to represent the Netherlands and was subsequently called up for the Netherlands' two final [[UEFA Euro 2016 qualifying]] matches, against [[Kazakhstan national football team|Kazakhstan]] on 10 October 2015 and the [[Czech Republic national football team|Czech Republic]] on 13 October.<ref>{{cite news|title=El Ghazi in line for Netherlands debut|url=https://int.soccerway.com/news/2015/October/02/el-ghazi-in-line-for-netherlands-debut/n662235/|access-date=2 October 2015|publisher=Soccerway}}</ref> He was named in the ''Oranje'''s starting line-up for both matches as the Dutch defeated Kazakhstan 2‚Äì1 but lost to the Czechs 3‚Äì2. The Netherlands failed to qualify for the [[UEFA Euro 2016|tournament proper]] after finishing fourth in their [[UEFA Euro 2016 qualifying Group A|qualifying group]].

On 14 May 2021, El Ghazi was called up by [[Frank de Boer]] and the [[Netherlands National Football Team|Netherlands]] to be part of the provisional squad for the upcoming [[UEFA Euro 2020]] tournament, making it his first call-up to the national team since 2015.<ref>{{cite news|title=El Ghazi named in provisional Netherlands squad for Euro 2020|url=https://www.avfc.co.uk/news/2021/may/El-Ghazi-named-in-provisional-Netherlands-squad-for-Euro-2020/|access-date=14 May 2021|publisher=Aston Villa}}</ref>
