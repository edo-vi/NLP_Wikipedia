'''''Betacoronavirus 1''''' is a species of [[coronavirus]] which infects humans and cattle.<ref name="ICTV">{{cite web|url=https://ictv.global/taxonomy|title=Virus Taxonomy: 2018b Release|date=March 2019|website=International Committee on Taxonomy of Viruses (ICTV)|language=en|format=html|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180304035352/https://talk.ictvonline.org/taxonomy/|archive-date=4 March 2018|accessdate=23 January 2020}}</ref><ref>{{Cite journal|last1=Lim|first1=Yvonne Xinyi|last2=Ng|first2=Yan Ling|last3=Tam|first3=James P.|last4=Liu|first4=Ding Xiang|date=2016-07-25|title=Human Coronaviruses: A Review of Virus–Host Interactions|journal=Diseases|volume=4|issue=3|pages=26|doi=10.3390/diseases4030026|issn=2079-9721|pmc=5456285|pmid=28933406|quote=See Table 1.|doi-access=free}}</ref> The infecting virus is an [[Viral envelope|enveloped]], [[Sense (molecular biology)|positive-sense]], [[RNA|single-stranded]] [[RNA virus]] and is a member of the genus ''[[Betacoronavirus]]'' and subgenus ''[[Embecovirus]].<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|url=https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/Taxonomy/Browser/wwwtax.cgi?mode=Undef&id=694003&lvl=3&keep=1&srchmode=1&unlock|title=Taxonomy browser (Betacoronavirus 1)|website=www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov|access-date=2020-02-29}}</ref>'' Like other embecoviruses, it has an additional shorter [[peplomer|spike]]-like surface protein called [[hemagglutinin esterase]] (HE) as well as the larger [[coronavirus spike protein]].<ref name=":03">{{Cite journal|last1=Woo|first1=Patrick C.
