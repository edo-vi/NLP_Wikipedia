'''Eugene Beauharnais "E. B." Nash''' (8 March 1838 â€“ 6 November 1917) was one of America's leading 19th-century [[homeopath]]s.

Born in [[Hillsdale, New York]], Nash graduated from [[Cleveland Homoeopathic Medical College]] in 1874.  He served as Professor of [[Homeopathic Materia Medica|Materia Medica]] in the [[New York Homeopathic Medical College]], and also taught at the [[Homoeopathic Hospital of London]].

In 1903 he became president of the [[International Hahnemannian Association]] (IHA).

He is best known as an author of books on homeopathy.  His obituary in ''The Homeopathic Recorder'' remembered him as, "one of the great teachers of medicine...[who] will live in his books and in the hearts of the many doctors he has helped to be better physicians," and stated, "There are a host of homoeopathic physicians in different parts of the world to-day that owe their success in healing the sick to the writings of Eugene B. Nash.<ref>''The Homeopathic Recorder'', Volume 33, 1918. p. 67</ref>
