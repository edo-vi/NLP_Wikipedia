'''Midwest College of Oriental Medicine''' is a [[Private university|private]] [[For-profit higher education in the United States|for-profit university]] focused on [[Oriental medicine]] with locations in [[Racine, Wisconsin]] and [[Skokie, Illinois]]. The college offers education and clinical training in [[acupuncture]] and Herbal medicine including Chinese [[herbalism|herbs]], ''tui na'' [[massage]], and [[nutrition]].

==History==
Midwest College of Oriental Medicine was established in Chicago in 1979.<ref name=MidwestHistory>{{cite web|url=http://www.acupuncture.edu/?page_id=118 |title=History of the Midwest College of Oriental Medicine |publisher=Midwest College of Oriental Medicine |accessdate=February 26, 2013}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ccaom.org/downloads/AOMintheUS.pdf |title=Acupuncture and Oriental Medicine in the United States |author=Lixin Huang |publisher=Council of Colleges of Acupuncture and Oriental Medicine |date=2005 |accessdate=February 26, 2013}}</ref> The college later expanded to Racine.<ref name=Milwaukee2003>{{cite news|title=Need for Needles|url=https://news.google.com/newspapers?id=QzIqAAAAIBAJ&sjid=tUIEAAAAIBAJ&pg=5114,8358957&dq=midwest+college+of+oriental+medicine&hl=en|newspaper=Milwaukee Journal Sentinel|date=January 20, 2003}}</ref>

==Academics==
Midwest College participated in the founding of the [[Council of Colleges of Acupuncture and Oriental Medicine]],{{citation needed|date=February 2013}} one of the parent organizations of the [[Accreditation Commission for Acupuncture and Herbal Medicine]] (ACAHM). Midwest College of Oriental Medicine has been [[higher education accreditation|accredited]] by ACAHM since 1993.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.acaom.org/find-a-school/ |title=Find A School |publisher=Accreditation Commission for Acupuncture and Oriental Medicine |accessdate=February 26, 2013 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20130122012423/http://www.acaom.org/find-a-school/ |archivedate=January 22, 2013 }}</ref> Since 1996, the school has been formally affiliated with [[Guangzhou University of Chinese Medicine]].<ref name=MidwestHistory/>

[[Master's degree]]s are awarded in Oriental medicine and acupuncture. The Oriental Medicine program takes at least three years to complete; graduates receive a master's degree.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.acupuncture.edu/?page_id=145 |title=Oriental Medicine |publisher=Midwest College of Oriental Medicine |accessdate=February 26, 2013}}</ref> The acupuncture program requires a minimum of 2.5 years.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.acupuncture.edu/?page_id=138 |title=Acupuncture |publisher=Midwest College of Oriental Medicine |accessdate=February 26, 2013}}</ref> In 2003, the school reported that its students had an average age of about 40 and that many had worked in other health-care fields, such as [[physical therapy]] or medicine.<ref name=GuineaPig/>

Acupuncture and other services are provided in the clinics in which Midwest College students practice their skills; services are free to veterans.<ref name=Milwaukee2003/><ref name=GuineaPig>{{cite news|title=Guinea pig discount: Bills shrink when rookies provide treatment |newspaper=Chicago Tribune |date=November 11, 2003 |author=Julie Deardorff |url=http://www.highbeam.com/doc/1G1-119458213.html|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160414003928/https://www.highbeam.com/doc/1G1-119458213.html|url-status=dead|archive-date=April 14, 2016|via=HighBeam Research}}</ref>
