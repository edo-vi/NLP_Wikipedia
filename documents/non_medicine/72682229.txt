"'''Kill All the Things'''" is a song by the band TENLo. The song's music video features actor [[Dustin Diamond]] portraying [[Harvey Weinstein]] and actress Kelly Cunningham portraying a [[nun]]. The video was directed by Joshua Mendez and filmed in [[Milwaukee, Wisconsin]], in 2017. The video received attention by major [[News media|news outlets]]. 

==Background==
The [[music video]] was filmed in [[Milwaukee, Wisconsin]], during the summer of 2017. Both members of band TENLo, Joey Zak and TomE LaBrosse, are from Milwaukee. The video was released to coincide with the release of the song "Kill All the Things".<ref name="USAToday">{{cite news |last1=Nelson |first1=James B. |title=Dustin Diamond, who played 'Screech,' portrays Harvey Weinstein in music video |url=https://www.usatoday.com/story/life/nation-now/2017/12/26/dustin-diamond-screech-plays-harvey-weinstein-music-video/982292001/ |access-date=January 6, 2023 |newspaper=[[USA Today]] |date=December 26, 2017}}</ref> Diamond had lived in the Milwaukee area since 2002. He lived in the suburb, [[Port Washington, Wisconsin|Port Washington]].<ref name="Levy"/> The video was directed by Joshua Mendez and stars actor [[Dustin Diamond]], best known for playing [[Samuel "Screech" Powers]] in the nineties television series ''[[Saved by the Bell]]''.<ref name="Billboard"/> The video was released on December 25, 2017.<ref name="fox"/>

==Plot==
The plot of the video for "Kill All the Things" was based on a [[Harvey Weinstein]] storyline.<ref name="TMZ">{{cite news |title=Harvey Weinstein Lookalike Stabbed to Death in New Music Video |url=https://www.tmz.com/2017/12/26/dustin-diamond-harvey-weinstein-music-video-stab-kill/ |access-date=January 6, 2023 |agency=EHM Productions, inc. |publisher=[[TMZ]] |date=December 26, 2017}}</ref> Zak and LaBrosse both appear in the video: Zak as a bell boy and LaBrosse as a bartender.<ref name="Billboard"/> In the video the Harvey Weinstein character is seen doing [[illegal drugs|drugs]] and drinking [[Whiskey|alcohol]]. Clearly [[Altered state of consciousness|under the influence]] of those intoxicants, he is then lured to a hotel room by a dark haired woman (played by Kelly Cunningham), who is revealed to be a [[nun]].<ref name="Billboard"/><ref name="fox">{{cite news |title=Dustin Diamond stars as Harvey Weinstein in graphic new music video |url=https://www.foxnews.com/entertainment/dustin-diamond-stars-as-harvey-weinstein-in-graphic-new-music-video |access-date=January 6, 2023 |newspaper=[[New York Post]] |publisher=[[Fox News]] |date=December 26, 2017}}</ref> In the hotel room Weinstein is on his back laying on a bed when he is stabbed to death by the nun in a gruesome scene.<ref name="Billboard">{{cite magazine |last1=Hussein |first1=Wandera |title='Saved By The Bell' Actor Dustin Diamond Plays Disgraced Film Exec Harvey Weinstein in New Video |url=https://www.billboard.com/music/rock/dustin-diamond-harvey-weinstein-tenlo-kill-all-the-things-video-8084981/ |access-date=January 6, 2023 |magazine=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|agency=[[Penske Media Corporation]] |publisher=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard Media LLC]] |date=December 27, 2017}}</ref>

==Reception==
The video was covered by major news outlets like ''[[USA Today]]'', ''[[TMZ]]'', and ''[[The New York Times]]''. On [[YouTube]] the video was viewed more than 100,000 times.<ref name="Levy">{{cite news |last1=Levy |first1=Piet |title=In one of his final roles, Dustin Diamond channels Doc Brown for Milwaukee band TENLo's new music video |url=https://www.jsonline.com/story/entertainment/music/2021/02/04/one-his-final-roles-dustin-diamond-channels-doc-brown-tenlos-music-video/4376616001/ |access-date=January 6, 2023 |newspaper=[[Milwaukee Journal Sentinel]] |date=February 4, 2021}}</ref>
