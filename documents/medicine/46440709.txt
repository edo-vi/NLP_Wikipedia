'''''Mycoplasma adleri''''' is a species of bacteria in the genus ''Mycoplasma''. This [[genus]] of [[bacteria]] lacks a [[cell wall]] around their [[cell membrane]].<ref name=Sherris>{{cite book | author = Ryan KJ, Ray CG (editors) | title = Sherris Medical Microbiology | pages=409–12 |edition = 4th | publisher = McGraw Hill | year = 2004 | isbn = 0-8385-8529-9}}</ref> Without a cell wall, they are unaffected by many common [[antibiotics]] such as [[penicillin]] or other [[beta-lactam]] antibiotics that target cell wall synthesis. Mycoplasma are the smallest bacterial cells yet discovered,<ref>{{cite book|title=Infectious Diseases of the Female Genital Tract |url=https://archive.org/details/infectiousdiseas00swee_0 |url-access=registration | publisher=Lippincott Williams & Wilkins, 2009| author = Richard L. Sweet, Ronald S. Gibbs|year=1985 |isbn=9780683080384 }}</ref> can survive without oxygen and are typically about 0.1&nbsp;[[µm]] in diameter.  It is found in goats where it causes infection.<ref name="Del-GiudiceRose1995">{{cite journal|last1=Del Giudice|first1=R. A.|last2=Rose|first2=D. L.|last3=Tully|first3=J. G.|title=''Mycoplasma adleri'' sp. nov., an Isolate from a Goat|journal=International Journal of Systematic Bacteriology|volume=45|issue=1|year=1995|pages=29–31|issn=0020-7713|doi=10.1099/00207713-45-1-29|url=http://ijs.sgmjournals.org/content/45/1/29.full.pdf+html|pmid=7857804|doi-access=free}}</ref><ref name="MercierPellet2007">{{cite journal|last1=Mercier|first1=Pascale|last2=Pellet|first2=Marie-Pierre|last3=Morignat|first3=Eric|last4=Calavas|first4=Didier|last5=Poumarat|first5=François|title=Prevalence of mycoplasmas in external ear canal of goats: Influence of the sanitary status of the herd|journal=Small Ruminant Research|volume=73|issue=1–3|year=2007|pages=296–299|issn=0921-4488|doi=10.1016/j.smallrumres.2007.01.018}}</ref>
The type strain is strain G145 = ATCC 27948 = CIP 105676.<ref name="Del-GiudiceRose1995"/> Its genome has been determined.<ref name="HeldtanderPettersson1998">{{cite journal|last1=Heldtander|first1=M.|last2=Pettersson|first2=B.|last3=Tully|first3=J. G.|last4=Johansson|first4=K.-E.|title=Sequences of the 16S rRNA genes and phylogeny of the goat mycoplasmas ''Mycoplasma adleri'', ''Mycoplasma auris'', ''Mycoplasma cottewii'' and ''Mycoplasma yeatsii''|journal=International Journal of Systematic Bacteriology|volume=48|issue=1|year=1998|pages=263–268|issn=0020-7713|doi=10.1099/00207713-48-1-263|pmid=9542096|doi-access=free}}</ref>
''M. adleri'' is gram negative and appears round or coccobacillary in form.  Individual cells vary in diameter from 300 to 600&nbsp;nm, and each is surrounded by a single cytoplasmic membrane. The cell has a ‘fried-egg’ resemblance on a variety of growth media. It is anaerobic.<ref name="Del-GiudiceRose1995"/>
