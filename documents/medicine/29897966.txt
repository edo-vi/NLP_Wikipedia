{{Short description|Prion disease}}
'''Exotic ungulate encephalopathy''' is a [[transmissible spongiform encephalopathy]] (TSE), or [[prion]] [[disease]], identified in infected organs of [[zoo]] animals. This subgroup of the TSEs in captive animals was identified in zoo animals in [[Great Britain]] including species of [[greater kudu]], [[nyala]], [[gemsbok]], the [[common eland]], [[Arabian oryx|Arabian]] and [[scimitar oryx]], an [[Ankole-Watusi]] cow, and an American [[bison]].<ref name="TransfusionMicrobiology2008">{{cite book |author1=Contreras, Marcela |author2=Barbara, John A. Regan |title=Transfusion microbiology |publisher=Cambridge University Press |location=Cambridge, UK |year=2008 |pages= 141|isbn=978-0-521-45393-6 }}</ref> Studies indicate that transmission likely occurred via the consumption of feed supplemented with [[meat and bone meal]], although some animals died after the British ban on ground [[offal]] in animal feed.<ref name="Strauss2008">{{cite book |author1=Strauss, Ellen G. |author2=Strauss, James H. |title=Viruses and Human Disease |publisher=Elsevier / Academic Press |location=Boston, MA |year=2008 |isbn=978-0-12-373741-0 }}</ref><ref name="Baird-Parker2000">{{cite book |author1=Baird-Parker, A.
