'''Cinnamon tea''' is a [[herbal tea]] made by infusing [[cinnamon]] bark in water.<ref name="KF">{{Cite book|editor-last=The Korea Foundation|editor-link=The Korea Foundation|title=Korean Food Guide in English|date=2004|publisher=Cookand/Best Home Inc.|location=Seoul|isbn=89-89782-10-4|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=sfJFBAAAQBAJ&pg=PT51|accessdate=20 September 2017}}</ref>

== Regional variations ==

=== Chile ===
In [[Chile]], ''{{lang|es|té con canela}}'' ("tea with cinnamon") is made by placing a cinnamon stick into the teapot when steeping [[black tea]].<ref name="Campbell">{{Cite book|last1=Campbell|first1=Dawn L.|title=The tea book|date=1995|publisher=[[Pelican Publishing Company|Pelican Pub. Co.]]|location=Gretna, LA|isbn=1-56554-074-3|page=107|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=IS8XrKTH1BcC&pg=PA107|accessdate=20 September 2017}}</ref>

=== Korea ===
[[File:Cassia bark.jpg|thumb|[[Cinnamomum cassia|Cassia cinnamon]] barks used to make ''gyepi-cha'']]
''Gyepi-cha'' ({{Korean|hangul=계피차|hanja=桂皮茶|labels=no}}; "cinnamon tea") is a [[traditional Korean tea]] made from [[Cinnamomum cassia|cassia cinnamon]] barks.<ref name="DE">{{Cite web|url=http://www.doopedia.co.kr/doopedia/master/master.do?_method=view&MAS_IDX=101013000826781|title=Gyepi-cha|website=[[Doopedia]]|publisher=[[Doosan Corporation]]|language=ko|script-title=ko:계피차|access-date=20 September 2017}}</ref> Thicker sticks of cinnamon with purplish-red cross-section and strong fragrance are used.<ref name="DE" /> Dried cinnamon sticks are simmered either whole or sliced with a small amount of [[ginger]].<ref name="KF" /> When served, the tea is strained and optionally sweetened with sugar or honey, and then is usually garnished with minced [[jujube]]s.<ref name="KF" /><ref name="DE" />

=== Lebanon ===
In [[Lebanon]], ''shaayi bil qirfah wa’l yansoon’'' ("anise and cinnamon tea") is made by boiling [[aniseed]] and cinnamon (powdered or sticks) in water.<ref name="Iyengar">{{cite news|last=Iyengar|first=Krishnaraj|title=The world in a teacup: brews that hit the spot|url=http://www.indulgexpress.com/culture/cover-story/2017/jul/21/saucers-full-of-secrets-brews-that-can-hit-the-spot-2765.html|accessdate=20 September 2017|work=[[The New Indian Express]]|date=21 July 2017}}</ref> Optionally, black tea may be added.<ref name="Iyengar" /> The tea is strained and served with or without added sugar.<ref name="Iyengar" />
