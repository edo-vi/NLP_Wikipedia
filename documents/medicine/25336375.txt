'''Henry William Steele-Bodger''' [[MRCVS]] (1896 &ndash; 1952) was a British [[veterinary surgeon]].

Educated at [[Cranleigh School]], he served with the [[Royal Engineers]] and [[Royal Horse Artillery]]. He lost an eye in his war service.  After the war he qualified as a vet at the [[Royal School of Veterinary Studies|Royal Veterinary College]], in Edinburgh and set up practice in [[Tamworth, Staffordshire]]. He was active in leading vets and co-founded the Society of Veterinary Practitioners, together with Sir [[Thomas Dalling]].<ref name=":0">{{cite web|last=|first=|date=|title=HENRY WILLIAM (HARRY) STEELE-BODGER, MRCVS PRESIDENT NVMA â€“ 1939-1941 BIOGRAPHY|url=http://www.bva.co.uk/public/documents/hsb_biography.pdf|url-status=dead|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20111002021754/http://www.bva.co.uk/public/documents/hsb_biography.pdf|archivedate=2011-10-02|accessdate=2009-12-06|website=[[British Veterinary Association]]}} </ref> 

In 1940, he  served as President of the British Veterinary Association <ref>Obituary of Mary Brancker, the first female president of the British Veterinary Association), ''[[The Daily Telegraph]]'', 31 July 2010.</ref>

==Family==
His sons [[Micky Steele-Bodger|Micky]] and [[Alasdair Steele-Bodger|Alasdair]] were also veterinarians.
