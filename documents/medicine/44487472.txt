'''Esocid lymphosarcoma''', also known as '''''Esox'' lymphosarcoma'''<ref name="Plumb 2011" /> is a transmissible tumor which affects two species of fish, [[northern pike]] (''Esox lucius'') and [[Muskellunge]] (''Esox masquinongy''), in North America and Europe.<ref name="Coffee 2013">{{cite journal|last1=Coffee|first1=LL|last2=Casey|first2=JW|last3=Bowser|first3=PR|title=Pathology of tumors in fish associated with retroviruses: a review|journal=Veterinary Pathology|date=May 2013|volume=50|issue=3|pages=390â€“403|doi=10.1177/0300985813480529|pmid=23456970|doi-access=free}}{{open access}}</ref> The tumors initially are found in the skin, but later in the course of the disease are also found in the internal organs.<ref name="Plumb 2011"/> The tumors appear as colorless skin protrusions which are several centimeters in diameter.<ref>{{cite book|last1=Roberts|first1=Ronald J.|title=Fish Pathology|date=2012|publisher=John Wiley & Sons|isbn=1118222962|page=288|edition=4}}</ref> A [[retrovirus]] has been detected in affected cells by [[Electron microscope|electron microscopy]].<ref name="Plumb 2011">{{cite book|last1=Plumb|first1=John A.|last2=Hanson|first2=Larry A.|title=Health Maintenance and Principal Microbial Diseases of Cultured Fishes|url=https://archive.org/details/healthmaintenanc00plum|url-access=limited|date=2011|publisher=John Wiley & Sons|isbn=9780470958469|pages=[https://archive.org/details/healthmaintenanc00plum/page/n273 260]-261|edition=3rd|chapter=''Esox'' lymphosarcoma}}</ref> The disease is spread by physical contact between fish,<ref name="Plumb 2011"/> probably during the spring spawning season.<ref name="Coffee 2013" /> The disease has the lowest prevalence in the summer.<ref name="Coffee 2013" />
