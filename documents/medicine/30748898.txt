'''Viral arthritis''' is an infectious disease in [[poultry]], such as [[chickens]] and [[Turkey (bird)|turkeys]], caused by [[Avian reovirus]].<ref name="Merck 2020">{{cite web |last1=Nicholds |first1=Jenny |last2=Sellers |first2=Holly S. |title=Viral arthritis in poultry |url=https://www.merckvetmanual.com/poultry/viral-arthritis/viral-arthritis-in-poultry?query=viral%20arthritis |website=[[Merck Veterinary Manual]] |access-date=21 August 2021 |date=March 2020}}</ref> Arthritis and tenosynovitis are the main signs of Avian reovirus infection in chickens, although the virus can cause other signs.<ref name="Jones 2000">{{cite journal |last1=Jones |first1=R.C. |title=Avian reovirus infections. |journal=Revue Scientifique et Technique (International Office of Epizootics) |date=August 2000 |volume=19 |issue=2 |pages=614–25 |doi=10.20506/rst.19.2.1237 |doi-access= |pmid=10935283}}</ref>

The prominent sign is swelling of the digital flexor and metatarsal extensor [[tendon]]s. The [[hock (anatomy)|hock]] joint itself is not so sharply affected, showing just a small amount of [[synovial fluid|synovial]] [[exsudate]] when opened.<ref>{{cite book |last=Olson |first=N.O. |chapter=Reovirus infections |title=Diseases of poultry |editor-first=M.S. |editor-last=Hofstad |publisher=Iowa State University Press |location=Ames, Iowa, USA |ISBN=0-8138-0430-2 |pages=560–566}}</ref>

Vaccination is the primary method used to control this disease.<ref name="Jones 2000" />
