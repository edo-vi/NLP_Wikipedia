The '''International Sanitary Convention for Aerial Navigation (1933)''' was an [[International Sanitary Conferences|international sanitary convention]], drawn up in 1932 and signed at [[The Hague]] on 12 April 1933 (without a conference) and came into force on 1 August 1935 to protect communities against diseases liable to be imported by [[aircraft]] and to protect [[air crew]] against diseases due to flying. It contained a number of regulations consisting of measures to prevent the spread of [[Plague (disease)|plague]], [[cholera]], [[yellow fever]], [[typhus]] and [[smallpox]].
