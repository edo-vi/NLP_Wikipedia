{{short description|Field of alternative medicine}}
'''Longevity medicine''' is a set of [[preventive healthcare]] practices that rely on [[biomarkers of aging]], such as [[aging clock]]s, to keep the patient's biological and psychological age as near to peak performance as feasible throughout life.<ref name=":0">{{Cite journal|last1=Bischof|first1=Evelyne|last2=Scheibye-Knudsen|first2=Morten|last3=Siow|first3=Richard|last4=Moskalev|first4=Alexey|date=2021-04-01|title=Longevity medicine: upskilling the physicians of tomorrow|journal=The Lancet Healthy Longevity|language=English|volume=2|issue=4|pages=e187–e188|doi=10.1016/S2666-7568(21)00024-6|pmid=36098117 |s2cid=233535190|issn=2666-7568|doi-access=free}}</ref><ref name=":1">{{Cite web|last=Holl|first=Brenna|date=2021-11-28|title=These Are the Core Concepts of Longevity Medicine|url=https://www.wellandgood.com/longevity-medicine/|access-date=2022-01-11|website=Well+Good|language=en-US}}</ref> [[Biogerontology]] and [[precision medicine]] are some of the related fields.<ref name=":0" /> As of early 2020s it is a "fast developing field", according to an article in a ''Lancet'' specialty journal.<ref name=":0" />

{{Alternative medicine sidebar}}
In the first decade of the 21st century, what was called "'''age management medicine'''" was considered a field of [[alternative medicine]],<ref>{{cite journal |last1=Walker |first1=Richard F |title=Challenges facing age-management/longevity medicine |journal=Clinical Interventions in Aging |date=2007 |volume=2 |issue=2 |pages=175–177 |pmid=18044133 |pmc=2684514 }}</ref> and, as of 2007, was not recognized by the [[American Medical Association]].<ref name=CNN2007/> Other names at this time included "antiaging medicine" and "regenerative medicine".<ref name=SF2006/> Age management medicine is controversial.<ref name=CNN2007>{{cite news |title='Age management' is a controversial new medical focus |url=https://www.cnn.com/2007/HEALTH/04/06/chasing.antiaging.med/index.html |work=CNN |date=May 9, 2007 }}</ref> The field is underregulated and supported by insufficient scientific evidence. People who practice it open themselves up to legal liability on grounds of negligence–malpractice, warranty issues, and product liability.<ref>{{cite journal |last1=Reisman |first1=Neal R. |title=Anti-Aging Medicine: The Legal Issues: Legal Issues Associated With the Current and Future Practice of Anti-Aging Medicine |journal=The Journals of Gerontology: Series A |date=1 July 2004 |volume=59 |issue=7 |pages=B674–B681 |doi=10.1093/gerona/59.7.B674 |pmid=15304531 |doi-access=free }}</ref> The use of [[growth hormone]] has been frequently recommended; however, such use is associated with [[cancer]].<ref name=SF2006>{{cite news |last1=Russell |first1=Sabin |last2=Writer |first2=Chronicle Medical |title=Cancer took life of noted user of growth hormone |url=https://www.sfgate.com/health/article/Cancer-took-life-of-noted-user-of-growth-hormone-2533392.php |work=SFGate |date=8 June 2006 }}</ref>
Age management medicine is often promoted by anti-aging practitioners specializing in nutritional supplements and hormone-replacement, a practice that may lead to harmful side-effects.<ref name="CNN2016">{{cite news |title=The Risks of Anti-aging Medicine |url=https://www.cnn.com/2011/12/28/health/age-youth-treatment-medication/index.html |work=CNN |date=December 14, 2016}}</ref>
