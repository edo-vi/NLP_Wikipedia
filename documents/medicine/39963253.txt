'''''Science-Based Medicine''''' is a website and blog with articles covering issues in [[science]] and [[medicine]], especially [[quackery|medical scams and practices]].<ref name="McNamee">{{cite news |last1=McNamee |first1=David |date=August 22, 2014 |title=Why is scientific literacy among the general population important? |work=[[Medical News Today]] |url=http://www.medicalnewstoday.com/articles/281470.php |access-date=August 23, 2016 |archive-date=August 17, 2016 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160817131040/http://www.medicalnewstoday.com/articles/281470.php |url-status=live }}</ref> Founded in 2008, it is owned and operated by the [[New England Skeptical Society]]<ref name=":5" /> and run by [[Steven Novella]] and [[David Gorski]].<ref name=":6">{{Cite web |last=Williams |first=Nathan |date=2021-08-25 |title=Ideology-based medicine |url=https://thecritic.co.uk/ideology-based-medicine/ |access-date=2022-06-26 |website=[[The Critic (modern magazine)|The Critic]] |language=en-GB |archive-date=June 27, 2022 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20220627033152/https://thecritic.co.uk/ideology-based-medicine/ |url-status=live }}</ref>

== Content and format ==
''Science-Based Medicine'' is a website in blog format that examines controversies in science and medicine,<ref name="Johannes">{{cite news |last1=Johannes |first1=Laura |date=May 19, 2014 |title=Will Getting Grounded Help You Sleep Better and Ease Pain? |work=[[Wall Street Journal]] |url=https://www.wsj.com/articles/SB10001424052702304652804579572072977644460 |access-date=August 23, 2016 |quote=says Steven Novella, a clinical neurologist at Yale University School of Medicine in New Haven, Conn., and executive editor of the blog Science-Based Medicine, which looks at controversies in science and medicine. |archive-date=August 28, 2016 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160828032740/http://www.wsj.com/articles/SB10001424052702304652804579572072977644460 |url-status=live }}</ref> especially [[Quackery|medical scams and practices]].<ref name="McNamee" /> SBM is known for persistently challenging [[alternative medicine]]<ref name=":2">{{Cite journal |last=Freedman |first=David H. |date=July 2011 |title=The Triumph of New-Age Medicine |url=https://www.theatlantic.com/magazine/archive/2011/07/the-triumph-of-new-age-medicine/308554/ |journal=The Atlantic |quote=Novella is a highly respected Yale neurologist, and the editor of Science-Based Medicine, an influential blog that has tirelessly gone after alternative medicine. |access-date=March 11, 2017 |archive-date=May 10, 2015 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150510142230/http://www.theatlantic.com/magazine/archive/2011/07/the-triumph-of-new-age-medicine/308554/ |url-status=live }}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |last=Horgan |first=John |author-link=John Horgan (journalist) |date=May 16, 2016 |title=Dear "Skeptics," Bash Homeopathy and Bigfoot Less, Mammograms and War More |url=https://blogs.scientificamerican.com/cross-check/dear-skeptics-bash-homeopathy-and-bigfoot-less-mammograms-and-war-more/ |access-date=2022-06-26 |website=Scientific American |language=en |archive-date=July 6, 2022 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20220706035400/https://blogs.scientificamerican.com/cross-check/dear-skeptics-bash-homeopathy-and-bigfoot-less-mammograms-and-war-more/ |url-status=live }}</ref><ref name="Painter" /> and for opposing university funding from advocates of [[Integrative Medicine|integrative medicine]].<ref>{{Cite web |last=Seltzer |first=Rick |date=September 26, 2017 |title=UC Irvine under scrutiny for taking $200 million for school of health from couple some say back junk science |url=https://www.insidehighered.com/news/2017/09/26/uc-irvine-under-scrutiny-taking-200-million-school-health-couple-some-say-back-junk |access-date=2022-06-26 |website=Inside Higher Education |language=en |archive-date=June 24, 2022 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20220624091641/https://www.insidehighered.com/news/2017/09/26/uc-irvine-under-scrutiny-taking-200-million-school-health-couple-some-say-back-junk |url-status=live }}</ref> David Freedman, writing for ''[[The Atlantic]]'' in 2011, described SBM as "an influential blog that has tirelessly gone after alternative medicine."<ref name=":2" />

Editorial staff say that the best medicine is based on [[scientific principles]], includes prior plausibility, and is not based on [[evidence]] alone.<ref name="SBMeditors" /> Gorski, Novella, and Atwood have argued that science-based medicine differs in focus from [[evidence-based medicine]]<ref name=":3">{{Cite web |date=August 20, 2014 |title=Experts denounce clinical trials of unscientific, 'alternative' medicines |url=https://www.sciencedaily.com/releases/2014/08/140820123248.htm |access-date=2022-06-26 |website=ScienceDaily |publisher=[[Cell Press]] |language=en |archive-date=June 27, 2022 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20220627031645/https://www.sciencedaily.com/releases/2014/08/140820123248.htm |url-status=live }}</ref><ref name="Lilienfeld" /> and stress that [[randomized clinical trials]] should only be conducted when warranted by ample preclinical evidence to justify the effort, time, and expenses involved.<ref name=":3" /> For a science-based approach, Novella supports minimizing or eliminating research on implausible treatments, and points out that decades are often required for clinical research to become supported by rigorous, conclusive trials, during which time decisions must be made, preferably guided by and screened by plausibility criteria.<ref name=":4" />

In a systematic survey of web sites providing material on [[complementary and alternative medicine]] from 2018, medical education researcher Annie Chen and colleagues listed ''Science-Based Medicine'' alongside ''[[WebMD]]'' as an example of an "information service" providing articles on health and illness.<ref name="chen">{{cite journal |vauthors=Chen AT, Taylor-Swanson L, Buie RW, Park A, Conway M |title=Characterizing Websites That Provide Information About Complementary and Integrative Health: Systematic Search and Evaluation of Five Domains |journal=Interact J Med Res |volume=7 |issue=2 |pages=e14 |date=October 2018 |pmid=30305254 |pmc=6231734 |doi=10.2196/ijmr.9803 |url= |doi-access=free }}</ref>

During the [[COVID-19 pandemic]], ''Science-Based Medicine'' collected and debunked misinformation that had spread through social media, such as the false claim that COVID-19 vaccines could cause infertility.<ref>{{cite journal |vauthors=Weinzierl MA, Harabagiu SM |date=December 2021 |title=Automatic detection of COVID-19 vaccine misinformation with graph link prediction |url= |journal=J Biomed Inform |volume=124 |issue= |pages=103955 |doi=10.1016/j.jbi.2021.103955 |pmc=8598278 |pmid=34800722}}</ref>

== Legal ==
{{further|Etanercept#Patents}}
In 2014, Novella was sued by Edward Tobinick,<ref name=":1" /> a doctor claiming to treat neurological conditions, over two blog posts on ''Science-Based Medicine'' critical of [[off-label use]] of the drug Etanercept by Tobinick's medical clinic.<ref>{{Cite web |last1=Kaplan |first1=Alex |last2=Pang |first2=Evelyn |date=2017-03-31 |title=No Scrubs Permitted: Eleventh Circuit Affirms Blog Post Is Not Advertising Actionable Under Lanham Act |url=https://www.lexology.com/library/detail.aspx?g=beecda8a-e47e-422e-8947-f390b493c13b |access-date=2022-06-27 |website=Lexology |language=en |archive-date=August 19, 2023 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20230819103822/https://www.lexology.com/library/detail.aspx?g=beecda8a-e47e-422e-8947-f390b493c13b |url-status=live }}</ref> Novella had said that it was "unethical for physicians to practice outside of their area of competence and expertise". The lawsuit, filed by Tobinick against Novella, the Society for Science-Based Medicine, Inc., and SGU Productions, LLC was resolved after the court ruled in favor of the defendants.<ref>{{Cite web |date=2017-02-24 |title=Another Free Speech Win In Libel Lawsuit Disguised As A Trademark Complaint |url=https://abovethelaw.com/2017/02/another-free-speech-win-in-libel-lawsuit-disguised-as-a-trademark-complaint/ |access-date=2022-06-27 |website=Above the Law |language=en-US |archive-date=August 31, 2023 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20230831221105/https://abovethelaw.com/2017/02/another-free-speech-win-in-libel-lawsuit-disguised-as-a-trademark-complaint/ |url-status=live }}</ref>
