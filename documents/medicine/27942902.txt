{{short description|Protozoan disease of animals}}
'''Besnoitiosis''' is a [[protozoa]]n disease of the skin, [[subcutis]], blood vessels, mucous membranes, and other tissues" of animals.<ref name="Merck">{{cite web|title=Besnoitiosis: Introduction|url=http://www.merckvetmanual.com/mvm/index.jsp?cfile=htm/bc/50500.htm|website=Merck Veterinary Manual}}</ref> It "is endemic in tropical and sub-tropical regions with high infection rate but low mortality"; however, it is rare in other regions.<ref name="Free">{{cite web|title=Besnoitiosis|url=http://medical-dictionary.thefreedictionary.com/besnoitiosis|website=The Free Dictionary}}</ref>

The causative organisms of the cutaneous disease, and the affected animals include:<ref name="Merck"/><ref name="Free"/>
* ''[[Besnoitia besnoiti]]'' in [[cattle]] (swelling of the lymph nodes, subcutaneous swellings, diarrhea, abortion and infertility)
* ''[[Besnoitia bennetti]]'' in [[horse]]s and [[donkey]]s (characterized by a widespread, serious dermatitis)
* ''[[Besnoitia jellisoni]]''  and ''[[Besnoitia wallacei|B. wallacei]]'' in [[rodent]]s
* ''[[Besnoitia tarandi]]'' in [[reindeer]] and [[caribou]]
* ''[[Besnoitia darlingi]]'' in [[lizard]]s, [[opossum]]s, and [[snake]]s
* ''[[Besnoitia sauriana]]'' in lizards
* Viscerotropic strains of ''[[Besnoitia besnoiti|B.besnoiti]]'' in African antelope
* An unidentified ''[[Besnoitia]]'' species in [[goat]]s in [[Iran]], [[New Zealand]], and [[Kenya]]
