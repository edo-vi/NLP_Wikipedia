'''''Gugi-cha''''' ({{Korean|hangul=구기차|hanja=枸杞茶|labels=no}}) or '''goji tea''' is a traditional Chinese/Korean tea made from dried [[Lycium chinense|goji]] berries or leaves.<ref name="Doo">{{Cite web|url=https://www.doopedia.co.kr/doopedia/master/master.do?_method=view&MAS_IDX=101013000717281|title=Gugi-cha|website=[[Doopedia]]|publisher=[[Doosan Corporation]]|language=ko|script-title=ko:구기차|access-date=21 August 2017}}</ref> Traditionally, the tea was made with young [[Lycium chinense|goji]] leaves.<ref name="Mun">{{Cite web|url=http://encykorea.aks.ac.kr/Contents/Index?contents_id=E0005676|title=Gugi-cha|last=문|first=범수|website=[[Encyclopedia of Korean Culture]]|publisher=[[Academy of Korean Studies]]|language=ko|script-title=ko:구기차|access-date=21 August 2017}}</ref> Today, mature leaves or, more commonly, berries are used.<ref name="Doo" /><ref name="Mun" /> The tea made with berries may be called '''''gugija-cha''''' ({{Korean|hangul=구기자차|hanja=枸杞子茶|labels=no}}) or '''goji berry tea''', while the tea made with leaves is referred to as '''''gugiyeop-cha''''' ({{Korean|hangul=구기엽차|hanja=枸杞葉茶|labels=no}}) or '''goji leaf tea'''.<ref name="Doo" />

== Preparation ==
Tea using berries is prepared with around {{Convert|20-25|g|abbr=on}} of dried goji berries simmered in two cups of water, wth various possible flavorings or sweeteners added.<ref name="Mun" /> Leaf tea may be prepared with around {{Convert|2-3|g|abbr=on}} of dried leaves  infused in a cup of hot water.<ref name="Mun" />
