'''Edward Cornock Straiton''' [[OBE]] (27 March 1917 â€“ 30 October 2004),<ref>''England & Wales, Civil Registration Death Index, 1916-2007''</ref> known as "the [[Television|TV]] vet", was a Scottish [[veterinarian]], author, and television host.<ref name=independent>{{cite news |title=Obituary: Eddie Straiton |url=https://www.independent.co.uk/news/obituaries/eddie-straiton-532634.html |work=The Independent |date=10 November 2004| accessdate=18 November 2020}}</ref> He was the studio-based technical advisor for the BBC television series ''[[All Creatures Great and Small (1978 TV series)|All Creatures Great and Small]]''.<ref>[http://www.bbc.co.uk/stoke/news/2004/10/301004.shtml BBC News] Stoke & Staffordshire News - Day By Day Archives - Friday 5 November</ref><ref name=independent/>

Born at [[Clydebank]], [[Dunbartonshire]], Straiton studied at [[University of Glasgow|Glasgow veterinary school]]. He started his veterinarian practice in the 1940s and practised in [[Penkridge]], [[Staffordshire]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.straitonvets.co.uk/about/|title=About E.C. Straiton & Partners Veterinary Practice|accessdate=18 November 2020}}</ref> He began his TV career serving as a host on the television show ''[[Farming Today]]'' in 1957, giving advice on animal health. His broadcasting reached its peak audience when, in 1977, he took up a regular slot on [[BBC Radio]]'s ''[[Jimmy Young Show]]''. He also wrote a series of popular veterinary books (by "the TV Vet") on farm animals and domestic pets. The books sold almost a million copies worldwide.<ref name=independent/>

He was appointed an Officer of the [[Order of the British Empire]] (OBE) in the [[1998 New Year Honours]], for services to the veterinary profession and to agriculture.<ref>{{London Gazette |issue=54993 |date= 30 December 1997| page=13 |supp=y}}</ref><ref name="independent"/>
