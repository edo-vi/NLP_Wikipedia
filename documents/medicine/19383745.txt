'''Propagermanium''' ([[International Nonproprietary Name|INN]]), also known by a variety of other names including '''bis(2-carboxyethylgermanium) sesquioxide''' and '''2-carboxyethylgermasesquioxane''', is an [[organometallic chemistry|organometallic]] compound of [[germanium]]<ref>M.P. Egorov, P.P. Gaspar (1994), ''Germanium: Organometallic chemistry'' in Encyclopedia of Inorganic Chemistry. John Wiley & Sons {{ISBN|0-471-93620-0}}</ref> that is sold as an [[alternative medicine]]. It is a polymeric compound with the formula ((HOOCCH<sub>2</sub>CH<sub>2</sub>Ge)<sub>2</sub>O<sub>3</sub>)<sub>''n''</sub>.

The compound was first synthesized in 1967 at the Asai Germanium Research Institute<ref>{{Cite web|title=Asai Germanium Japan|url=https://asaigermanium.com/|access-date=2022-02-04|website=Asai Germanium Japan|language=en-US}}</ref> in [[Japan]]. It is a water-soluble organogermanium compound used as raw material in health foods. The compound displays low toxicity in studies with rats.<ref>{{cite journal |last1=Doi |first1=Yuko|display-authors=etal|date=2017 |title=No carcinogenicity of poly-trans-[(2-carboxyethyl) germasesquioxane] (Ge-132): 26-week feeding study using rasH2 mice |journal=Fundamental Toxicological Sciences |volume=4 |issue=3 |pages=137â€“150 |doi=10.2131/fts.4.137 |doi-access=free }}</ref>
